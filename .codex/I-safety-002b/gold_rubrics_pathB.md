## #75 — Plasma metal-ion modulation as CVD prevention/therapy
*Clinical slice. Differentiator stress: separating a real-but-weak/contested signal from an*
*unproven one without overstating efficacy.*

Required elements (each → authoritative source class for Codex to verify + pin a gold span):
1. **Which metal ions are mechanistically implicated in CVD** (e.g. iron overload, copper, zinc,
   selenium, magnesium, calcium) and the direction of association — narrative/systematic review or
   AHA scientific statement.
2. **Proposed intervention TYPES** named in the literature: dietary/supplemental repletion
   (selenium, zinc, magnesium), and **chelation** (e.g. EDTA) to remove pro-oxidant metals —
   review + the chelation trial literature.
3. **Chelation therapy clinical evidence** — TACT (Trial to Assess Chelation Therapy, JAMA 2013)
   + TACT2; the signal is modest and contested, and **major guidelines do NOT endorse chelation
   for routine CVD prevention** (AHA). MUST be framed as weak/not-recommended, not as established.
4. **Selenium** supplementation evidence — RCT/meta-analysis (e.g. SU.VI.MAX, KiSel-10); mixed,
   benefit largely confined to deficient populations; not an established CVD therapy.
5. **Magnesium** — inverse observational association with CVD vs limited RCT outcome evidence;
   supplementation feasibility vs unproven hard-outcome efficacy — meta-analysis.
6. **Iron** — iron-overload/CVD-risk hypothesis and **iron-reduction (phlebotomy) RCT (FeAST,
   2007)** showing no overall CV event reduction — RCT.
7. **Overall honest verdict** — feasibility (supplements are deliverable/safe in deficiency) is
   established, but **hard-outcome efficacy for CVD is largely unproven/contested**; no metal-ion
   modulation is guideline-recommended as CVD therapy. (The faithfulness trap: claiming proven
   efficacy.)

## #76 — Gut microbiota, probiotics, prebiotics, pathogens, CRC, diet
*Clinical slice. Differentiator stress: precise consensus DEFINITIONS + correctly attributing*
*toxic-metabolite/pathogen claims, and not overstating human CRC-prevention evidence.*

1. **Predominant gut probiotic genera** — *Lactobacillus*, *Bifidobacterium* (and *Saccharomyces
   boulardii*, *Streptococcus/Enterococcus* where relevant) — ISAPP / major review.
2. **Consensus DEFINITION of probiotics** — ISAPP/WHO-FAO: "live microorganisms that, when
   administered in adequate amounts, confer a health benefit on the host" — ISAPP 2014 consensus.
3. **Consensus DEFINITION of prebiotics + mechanism** — ISAPP 2017: "a substrate selectively
   utilized by host microorganisms conferring a health benefit"; mechanism = selective
   fermentation → SCFAs; examples inulin/FOS/GOS — ISAPP 2017 consensus.
4. **SCFA mechanistic role** — butyrate/propionate/acetate; butyrate as colonocyte energy source +
   anti-inflammatory / Treg induction — review.
5. **Pathogenic bacteria of concern (gut/CRC)** — e.g. *Fusobacterium nucleatum*, enterotoxigenic
   *Bacteroides fragilis* (ETBF), *pks+ E. coli*, *C. difficile* — primary microbiome-CRC
   literature (Castellarin/Kostic 2012; ETBF; colibactin).
6. **Toxic metabolites + producing organism** — colibactin (pks+ *E. coli*, genotoxic), *B.
   fragilis* toxin (fragilysin/BFT), hydrogen sulfide, secondary bile acids, ammonia — primary
   literature. (Trap: mis-pairing metabolite ↔ organism.)
7. **Microbiota ↔ CRC link** — dysbiosis + *F. nucleatum*/ETBF enrichment in CRC; probiotics
   mitigating inflammation/progression is **largely preclinical — human prevention evidence is
   limited**, MUST be framed as such — review.
8. **Dietary optimization** — high-fiber/prebiotic intake, fermented foods, plant diversity,
   reduced red/processed meat → microbiome diversity; honest evidence-strength framing — guideline/
   review.

## #78 — Parkinson's disease: staging warning signs, family triggers, post-DBS support
*Clinical slice. Differentiator stress: correct stage attribution, device-safety precautions*
*(patient-harm if wrong), and not overstating DBS benefit.*

1. **Prodromal/early warning signs** — hyposmia, REM sleep behaviour disorder, constipation,
   depression; cardinal early motor signs (resting tremor, bradykinesia, rigidity) — MDS clinical
   diagnostic criteria / NICE NG71 / Parkinson's Foundation.
2. **Disease staging** — Hoehn & Yahr 1–5 (and/or MDS-UPDRS) with what each stage looks like —
   Hoehn & Yahr / MDS.
3. **Advanced-stage warning signs** — falls, freezing of gait, dysphagia (aspiration risk),
   cognitive decline/dementia, psychosis/hallucinations, autonomic dysfunction (orthostatic
   hypotension) — NICE / MDS.
4. **Family "seek medical advice" TRIGGERS** — new falls, swallowing difficulty/choking,
   hallucinations/confusion, sudden deterioration (infection/med change), motor fluctuations/
   dyskinesia, mood/suicidality, dopamine-agonist impulse-control disorders — Parkinson's
   Foundation caregiver guidance / NICE.
5. **DBS overview** — targets (STN/GPi), what it improves (motor fluctuations, dyskinesia, tremor)
   vs what it does NOT (axial/cognitive/non-motor) — landmark DBS RCT (Deuschl 2006 NEJM / EARLYSTIM
   2013 NEJM) + guideline.
6. **Post-DBS daily-life adjustments / device precautions** — MRI-conditional only, diathermy
   contraindicated, strong-EM-field/security-scanner caution, IPG/battery management, programming
   visits, medication reduction — device labeling (Medtronic) + guideline. (Patient-safety trap.)
7. **Post-DBS support strategies** — PT/OT/speech therapy, fall prevention, caregiver +
   psychological support, monitoring stimulation-induced effects (speech, mood, weight) — MDS /
   Parkinson's Foundation.
8. **Honest framing** — DBS improves QoL/motor symptoms but is **not a cure** and carries surgical
   risk — guideline.
