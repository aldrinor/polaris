## Findings
### P0
- Clinical safety is still not approvable. §5 says “GRADE-style” certainty per claim, but GRADE certainty depends on a body of evidence and domains like risk of bias, inconsistency, indirectness, imprecision, and publication bias; policy decisions also require benefits/harms, values, resources, equity, acceptability, and feasibility. Fix: define a real clinical evidence artifact: outcome-level Summary of Findings, absolute effects, harms, applicability, source hierarchy, downgrade reasons, and abstention thresholds. Sources checked: [CDC ACIP GRADE Handbook](https://www.cdc.gov/acip-grade-handbook/hcp/chapter-7-grade-criteria-determining-certainty-of-evidence/index.html), [GRADE EtD framework](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12961-018-0320-2).
- Regulatory/intended-use boundary is missing. “Clinical-grade” for a PM-office recipient cannot float without explicit intended user, intended/non-intended use, non-reliance language, knowns/unknowns, data quality requirements, evaluator validation, and Canadian-population applicability. Health Canada and FDA both emphasize clear information, logic/basis, validation, and independent review of CDS basis. Sources checked: [Health Canada MLMD guidance, Apr. 1 2026](https://www.canada.ca/en/health-canada/services/drugs-health-products/medical-devices/application-information/guidance-documents/pre-market-guidance-machine-learning-enabled-medical-devices.html), [Health Canada/FDA/MHRA transparency principles](https://www.canada.ca/en/health-canada/services/drugs-health-products/medical-devices/transparency-machine-learning-guiding-principles.html), [FDA CDS policy navigator](https://www.fda.gov/medical-devices/digital-health-center-excellence/step-6-software-function-intended-provide-clinical-decision-support).

### P1
- The “independent model family” proof risks being over-trusted. It is a strong product differentiator, but not clinical validation. Add evaluator accuracy metrics, disagreement handling, calibration examples, deterministic numeric checks, and a visible “what this verifier cannot prove” panel.
- Source-strategy controls can enable cherry-picking. §8 needs locked conservative defaults, visible deviations, sensitivity runs, and receipt-level recording of every inclusion/exclusion policy.
- The hero is close, but not yet clinical-grade. The replay must show two separate judgments: “text is supported by this span” and “the evidence is strong enough for this clinical/policy claim.” Without that separation it regresses to premium citation UI.
- Offline verification is not execution-safe yet. §11 needs key identity, signing authority, key rotation/revocation, pass/fail states, signature-missing copy, and a dry-run verifier script path.
- The PM demo script is only named. §14 should specify the actual 90-second walkthrough, including one verified claim, one partial/unsupported claim, one inadequacy refusal, and the offline receipt handoff.

### P2 / P3
- Add Canadian clinical source policy explicitly: CADTH, Health Canada labels/advisories, Canadian guidelines where available, and when non-Canadian evidence is acceptable.
- Keep WCAG/accessibility acceptance tied to real testing with assistive tech, not only axe. Canada’s digital standards call for accessibility from the start and user testing with people with disabilities. Sources checked: [Canada Digital Standards](https://www.canada.ca/en/government/system/digital-government/government-canada-digital-standards.html), [W3C WCAG 2.2](https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/).
- The live screenshots remain visually calm but not yet gift-grade; that is acceptable only because §13 explicitly calls for structural rebuilds.

## New-since-v1
- “Clinical-grade certainty” is now central, but the method is not defined enough to support the claim.
- “Two-family” is now promoted as a trust mechanism; it needs measured reliability and limits, or it becomes a new overclaim.
- Source controls are now user-editable; without guardrails they can undermine the audit story.
