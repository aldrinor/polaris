That does **not** refute the merge-key-is-sole-defense claim; it reinforces it. But it creates an execution risk: `verified_support_origin_count` must be computed by verifying the canonical claim against each basket member/span in isolation, not by counting all tokens from one multi-citation `SentenceVerification`. Add an explicit union-laundering test: one sentence/basket where the union passes but one member fails alone must render count `1`, not `2`.

P2: §8 test #1 is one-way as written. “Every key-tuple field is in `REQUIRED_KNOWN_*` or allowlist” prevents unguarded tuple fields, but does not by itself prove every required-known discriminator is actually included in the equality tuple. §4’s prose says the builder iterates the required-known set, which is the right fix; make the test bidirectional or generate the tuple directly from an ordered required-known spec.
