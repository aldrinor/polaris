**Decision: C + D.**

Implement both: a curated authoritative-source floor overlay plus content-signal fixes, and add an explicit MDPI/low-quality-OA ceiling. Do not relax adequacy thresholds.

A-only is too blunt for clinical safety. B-only will keep missing guideline bodies and society venues. D is mandatory because the current failure is bidirectional: it under-credits AHA/JACC/ESC/PMC while over-crediting MDPI.

**Guardrails**
Hard negative rules win before any floor: retracted papers, social/news/blog/vendor pages, industry marketing, low-provenance hosts, clinical reference products, student/predatory/low-quality venues.

Authoritative floors must be narrow and auditable:

- AHA/JAHA/Circulation, JACC, NEJM, Lancet, JAMA, BMJ, Cochrane, PubMed/PMC peer-reviewed content: floor to T1/T2 based on article type.
- ESC/ACC/AHA/NICE guideline paths: high-authority guideline floor, with reason text saying guideline authority, not “primary study.”
- ACC tools/dosing PDFs/practice-support assets do not get T1/T2 just because `acc.org`.
- MDPI and `10.3390` must not classify as T1/T2. Treat as T4 ceiling unless another explicit safety-reviewed exception exists.
- Unknown OpenAlex `article+journal` hosts remain T4, not T1.

One important correction: AHA rows currently hitting T7 are partly a content-length/stub issue. Do not let an authority floor silently launder unusable 297-character fetches into claim-supporting evidence. If the implementation keeps one `tier` field, tests must ensure content-starved authoritative sources are flagged and not used for sentence-level support unless full text/usable abstract exists.

**Test Invariants**
Assert:

- `ahajournals.org` primary article -> T1/T2, not T7 when usable content/metadata exists.
