### Mechanism Interpretation: Task-Based Frameworks and Generative AI

The verified findings establish a theoretical foundation rooted in the task-based model of production articulated by Acemoglu and Restrepo, in which production requires tasks allocated to capital or labor, and new technologies alter not only the productivity of existing task assignments but the very allocation of tasks between these factors of production [1]. [confidence: no-source-found — no grounded source was found for this statement; shown unverified] The framework's empirical relevance is demonstrated by the finding that approximately half of employment growth between 1980 and 2015 occurred in occupations where job titles or task content had changed—a statistic that underscores how technological change reconstitutes work from within rather than simply destroying or creating whole occupations [1]. [confidence: moderate — moderate confidence — verified against the cited source] Autor's complementary thesis provides the second theoretical pillar: the interplay between machine and human comparative advantage allows computers to substitute for workers in performing routine, codifiable tasks while amplifying the comparative advantage of workers who supply problem-solving skills, adaptability, and creativity [2]. [confidence: moderate — moderate confidence — verified against the cited source] This thesis has historically explained the observed polarization of the labor market, in which wage gains accrued disproportionately to those at the top and bottom of the skill distribution rather than to those in the middle [2]. [confidence: moderate — moderate confidence — verified against the cited source] Critically, Autor emphasizes the strong complementarities between automation and labor—productivity gains that raise earnings and augment demand for labor—suggesting that substitution effects are not the whole story [2]. [confidence: moderate — moderate confidence — verified against the cited source] Generative AI complicates this established framework in a fundamental way.
