**§-1.1 Audit Verdict**

Do not lock yet. [0a.-1.B](</C:/POLARIS/state/polaris_statistical_contract/v3_4_phase_0a_0/0a-1B_severity_fabrication_rubric.md:17>) is close and mostly directionally right, but three issues would make calibration unstable: the S0/S1 boundary is still too broad, fail-upward can silently become gold-label over-severity, and the fabrication taxonomy is missing a non-numeric relation/direction inversion type.

1. The §1.1 severity tree is not yet reproducible enough. “Direct, near-term, hard-to-reverse” is the right S0 concept, but the rubric does not say whether all three predicates must be met, what action context adjudicators should assume, or what evidence they must record for an S0 trigger. Two blinded adjudicators could diverge on the same false claim because “could cause legal liability / regulatory non-compliance” can be read narrowly as binding-action harm or broadly as any bad downstream consequence. The fix is small but important: require adjudicators to identify the acted-on decision, the concrete harm path, whether harm is direct/near-term/hard-to-reverse, and the S0 trigger category. If that path cannot be stated, the label should fall to S1 unless a domain anchor clearly controls.

2. The clinical anchors over-call S0. “Fabricated or inverted safety signal” is too broad as an automatic S0 anchor. The constipation/discontinuation case is definitely a `qualitative_negation` fabrication and clinically important, but it should not automatically be S0 merely because it negates a safety/tolerability fact. A false “no contraindication,” “no serious interaction,” “no boxed-warning risk,” or inversion of a serious safety outcome is S0. A low-frequency tolerability/discontinuation negation is usually S1 unless the packet’s decision context makes it likely to change prescribing, monitoring, discontinuation, or urgent care in a way that creates direct patient harm. Otherwise S0 prevalence will be inflated and calibration will teach SMEs that every safety-related falsehood is critical, which contradicts the §1.1 harm definition.

3. The non-clinical anchors are defensible at theme level but under-specified for blinded calibration. `policy`, `tech`, and `ai_sovereignty` are closest: binding legal obligations, safety/security properties, certification, data residency, export controls, and jurisdictional compliance are sensible S0 families. `due_diligence` needs to separate legal/compliance exposure from ordinary valuation harm; a fabricated metric that changes valuation is S1 unless it directly creates covenant, solvency, fraud, filing, or legal-action exposure. `canada_us` is too thin for the locked domain scope: it needs examples beyond trade obligation, at least immigration/status, defense/security, energy/interconnection, tariff/customs, and binding bilateral-agreement claims.

4. §1.3 fail-upward is correct as a first-pass safety discipline, but not sufficient as a gold-label discipline. As written, if both adjudicators are uncertain between S1 and S2, both mark S1, and consensus becomes S1 without escalation. That biases [Gate A](</C:/POLARIS/state/polaris_statistical_contract/v3_3/contract.md:31>) denominators because Gate A sizes and analyzes S1/S2 strata on adjudicated gold severity. Fix: `severity_uncertain=true` across a Gate A boundary should trigger tiebreak/panel review or at least require a recorded lower-candidate label and pre-outcome severity-migration audit. Fail-upward should protect triage; consensus gold should represent best adjudicated truth.

5. The §2 taxonomy is missing a seventh type: qualitative relation/direction inversion. Not every non-numeric inversion is a negation. Examples: “A outperformed B” when B outperformed A; “policy expands eligibility” when it narrows eligibility; “the rule permits export” when it prohibits export; “risk decreased” when the evidence says it increased. This is not cleanly `quantitative`, `qualitative_negation`, `entity_swap`, or `scope_overreach`. Add `relation_direction` or equivalent.

6. The six existing fabrication types are useful but need priority rules. `citation_swap` should mean “the proposition is true somewhere, but the cited source is wrong or nonexistent.” If the proposition itself is false, classify the factual mutation first and citation issue second. `temporal` should be primary only when the claim was once true or source-valid but is presented as current. `scope_overreach` should cover narrow-to-broad generalization, not every unsupported conclusion. Without these rules, D8 stratification by `fabrication_type` will be noisy.

7. The §3 injection protocol is basically sound: real-source-derived mutation, internal coherence, and label-symmetric packets match [0a.-1.A §5](</C:/POLARIS/state/polaris_statistical_contract/v3_4_phase_0a_0/0a-1A_sme_panel_role_governance.md:112>). The remaining leak risk is pattern leakage: if fabricated claims are always tidy one-token mutations and supported claims are not equivalently edited/matched, adjudicators can learn the construction style. Add a requirement for matched supported controls, identical paraphrase/editing passes, neutral facet generation, and no asymmetric contradiction spotlighting.

8. The §4 ground-truth model is correct. Consensus gold must govern; constructor intent is bookkeeping and construction-quality telemetry. Do not let constructor intent override blinded adjudication. But preserve the signal: record intent→gold migration by `fab_status`, severity, and fabrication type; use high disagreement rates to quarantine/top-up D8 cells before outcome unblinding. That keeps the gold set adjudicator-derived without discarding construction-quality information.

The artifact is buildable, but adjudicator calibration would likely fail at the S0/S1 boundary and fabrication-type boundary unless those fixes land first.

Fix §1.1/§1.2 S0/S1 operational triggers, §1.3 uncertainty-to-consensus handling for Gate A drift, and §2 taxonomy with a relation/direction type plus priority rules, then lock.
