# HARD CONSTRAINT CHANGE: zero human SMEs — best-effort human-free test+validate

## The operator directive (binding, 2026-05-27)

"We don't have money to hire anyone. What can we do for best effort to test and validate it. Stop telling me to hire anyone. Also tell it to Codex."

This is the SAME class of directive as the earlier "no external statistician — Claude+Codex work it out." Now: **NO human subject-matter experts (SMEs), ever. The ONLY budget is API fees.** The verifier safety validation must be doable with Claude + Codex + the POLARIS LLM stack + deterministic Python, and NOTHING human.

This is a binding HARD CONSTRAINT, not a preference. Do not propose hiring, recruiting, contracting, or "an external reviewer." If your design needs a human, the design is wrong for this constraint — find the human-free path.

## What this breaks (the locked design assumed humans)

The 14 locked Phase-0a.0 deliverables (state/polaris_statistical_contract/v3_4_phase_0a_0/) assume human SMEs in load-bearing places:
- **0a.-1.A** SME panel + role governance (constructor/adjudicator/tiebreaker = humans)
- **0a.-1.B** §4 ground-truth = human adjudicator consensus; first-pass blinded HUMAN labels
- **0a.-1.C** blinded_adjudication_labels + consensus_gold_labels = human-produced; severity_stratum derived from human consensus
- **Gate E** (contract v3.3 §7) = human inter-rater reliability (Gwet AC1 / Krippendorff on SME labels) — the VALIDITY gate that makes Gates A/B/D interpretable
- **D8** SME/template balancing + roster feasibility precheck
- **D6** sme_qualifications_required

These need rework for a human-free model.

## My proposed human-free architecture (pressure-test this)

**Split the two human jobs and replace each:**

### Job 1 — fabrication STATUS (is the claim fake?): make it MECHANICAL
- Build the gold set by taking REAL claims grounded in REAL fetched sources (prefer STRUCTURED sources where truth is a field value: clinical-trial registries NCT/EudraCT, drug labels with structured dose/contraindication fields, DOI'd papers with extractable numbers).
- PROGRAMMATICALLY inject fabrications by deterministic mutation (0a.-1.B's existing 7-type taxonomy): quantitative (5mg→15mg), entity_swap (drug A→drug B), qualitative_negation ("did"→"did not"), temporal (current→withdrawn), relation_direction (A>B→B>A). The ground truth "is this fabricated?" is MECHANICAL — we KNOW because we injected it. No human, and arguably more reliable than human fab-status labeling.
- citation_swap / scope_overreach are harder to make purely mechanical — flag which fab-types are mechanical vs need LLM-panel judgment.

### Job 2 — SEVERITY (how dangerous?): cross-family LLM panel + deterministic guardrails
- Severity (S0/S1/S2) is the irreducibly-judgment part. Human-free: a panel of DIFFERENT-FAMILY LLMs (e.g. Codex/GPT family + Claude family + the Qwen judge — distinct lineages so they don't share one blind spot), each applying the 0a.-1.B severity rubric, cross-checking.
- Deterministic guardrails where possible: a wrong dose in a structured drug-label field auto-flags S0-candidate; a mutation to a contraindication field auto-flags S0-candidate. The structured-source mutation TYPE constrains the plausible severity.
- DISCLOSE: severity is LLM-panel-adjudicated, NOT expert. The claim license forbids "expert-validated."

### Gate E reframed: cross-family LLM-judge agreement (not human IRR)
- Was: human inter-rater reliability. Now: agreement ACROSS the different-family LLM judges (AC1 / Krippendorff on their first-pass labels). Measures LLM-PANEL consistency, honestly labeled as such — NOT expert agreement.

### Independence / anti-circularity (the obvious objection)
- The risk: "LLMs checking LLMs." Defenses: (a) fab-STATUS ground truth is MECHANICAL (deterministic injection), not LLM-judged — so the core "did the verifier miss a fake?" measurement does NOT depend on an LLM judge; (b) two-family separation (generator family ≠ verifier family ≠ judge-panel families, per the contract's existing two-family rule); (c) deterministic Python checks (DOI/numeric/date) as an independent third leg; (d) §P3.4 LLM-as-judge leakage controls already locked.

### The honest claim (claim license must enforce)
- CAN claim: "verifier miss-rate bounded at X% on deterministically-injected fabrications against cited sources, severity cross-family-LLM-adjudicated."
- CANNOT claim: "expert-validated" / "clinically validated by physicians."

The split is the right direction, but I would tighten the terminology:

POLARIS can make **fabrication status** a true benchmark label through deterministic source-grounded mutation. POLARIS cannot make **clinical severity** fully ground truth without humans. It can make severity a **machine-adjudicated risk stratum** using structured-source guardrails plus cross-family LLM adjudication.

That distinction matters. The strongest honest design is:

- **Status label:** mechanical truth.
- **Mutation provenance:** deterministic and auditable.
- **Severity/risk stratum:** structured-rule constrained, LLM-panel adjudicated.
- **Claims license:** explicitly forbids “expert validated,” “clinically validated,” or equivalent language.

**1. Is This The Strongest Human-Free Design?**

Yes, with one improvement: do not let the LLM panel be the primary severity system when a deterministic proxy is available.

Use a three-layer design:

1. **Mechanical fabrication oracle**
   Real source claim → deterministic mutation → known fabricated/non-fabricated label.

2. **Deterministic hazard scaffold**
   The source field and mutation type assign a presumptive risk band where possible:
   dose, contraindication, boxed warning, withdrawal status, pediatric population, pregnancy warning, endpoint direction, mortality/safety outcome, etc.

3. **Cross-family LLM severity adjudication**
   Used for cases where structured rules cannot fully determine severity, or to confirm/challenge the deterministic hazard scaffold.

So the better framing is not “Job 1 mechanical, Job 2 LLM.” It is:

> Fabrication status is mechanical. Severity is mostly provenance-constrained and only partly LLM-adjudicated.

That is stronger because it reduces how much of the safety claim rests on LLM judgment.

**2. Mechanical Vs LLM-Panel Fabrication Types**

For the 7-type taxonomy, I would classify them like this.

**Mechanical ground truth, strong:**

- **Quantitative mutation**
  Example: `5 mg` → `15 mg`, `12%` → `21%`, `n=140` → `n=410`.
  Strong if the original value is parsed from a structured source or exact source text.

- **Entity swap**
  Example: drug A → drug B, trial ID A → trial ID B, disease X → disease Y.
  Strong if entities come from controlled vocabularies or structured fields.

- **Qualitative negation**
  Example: “reduced mortality” → “did not reduce mortality.”
  Strong when generated from known templates and the original polarity is explicit.

- **Temporal mutation**
  Example: active → withdrawn, completed → recruiting, current label → superseded label.
  Strong if sourced from dated registries, versioned labels, approval/withdrawal records, or trial status fields.

- **Relation direction**
  Example: A increased B → A decreased B; treatment superior to control → control superior to treatment.
  Strong when relation is template-controlled and the original relation is extractable.

**Mechanical ground truth, possible but needs careful fixture design:**

- **Citation swap**
  Mechanical if the cited source is programmatically replaced with a source that does not support the claim, while the original claim remains unchanged.
  Risk: “does not support” is sometimes semantic, not mechanical. This is strongest when the swapped citation is from a disjoint entity/trial/drug/population.

- **Scope overreach**
  Mechanical if generated from structured narrowing/widening.
  Example: source says “adults with condition X in trial Y” → claim says “all patients with condition X.”
  Risk: generalization boundaries are often semantic. This should be split into subtypes:
  - structured population overreach: mechanical
  - indication overreach: mechanical if label fields support it
  - causal/clinical recommendation overreach: LLM-panel judged

So I would not treat citation_swap and scope_overreach as uniformly non-mechanical. They are mechanical only when the unsupportedness is created by controlled source/field mismatch.

**3. Is Cross-Family LLM Severity Defensible?**

Defensible, but only if POLARIS calls it exactly what it is: **cross-family LLM severity adjudication**, not expert severity.

Two families is the minimum useful separation. Three is better if API budget allows. I would use:

- one GPT/Codex-family judge,
- one Claude-family judge,
- one non-aligned third family such as Qwen or another independently trained model available in the POLARIS stack.

Two-family agreement can catch some model-family-specific quirks. Three-family agreement lets you detect one-family outliers and compute more meaningful agreement statistics.

To reduce circularity:

- Keep generator, verifier, and judge roles separated by model family where feasible.
- Do not let judges see verifier outputs, rationales, scores, or failure labels.
- Freeze prompts, rubrics, model versions, decoding settings, and source packets before evaluation.
- Use deterministic Python to extract and compare numeric/date/identifier facts.
- Store judge first-pass labels before any consensus step.
- Include adversarial controls where the correct fabrication status is known but severity is intentionally ambiguous.
- Report disagreement, not just consensus.

The strongest protection is still this: **fabrication status is not judged by LLMs.** The LLM panel only affects severity/risk stratification.

**4. Does Gate E Still Mean Anything?**

Gate E no longer means what it used to mean.

Human Gate E used to ask:

> Are trained humans applying the label rubric consistently enough that Gates A/B/D are interpretable?

Human-free Gate E can only ask:

> Are the machine judges applying the severity/risk rubric consistently enough that severity-stratified results are stable?

That is weaker. It does not validate clinical truth. But it still has value if renamed and scoped correctly.

I would replace Gate E with two gates:

**Gate E1: Mechanical Oracle Integrity**

This becomes the validity backbone. It checks that the benchmark labels are actually known.

Examples:

- source fetch reproducibility,
- structured field extraction correctness,
- mutation audit logs,
- original-vs-mutated diff validity,
- no accidental unsupported original claims,
- deterministic expected label regeneration,
- fixture hash stability.

This is what makes Gates A/B/D interpretable for fabrication miss-rate.

**Gate E2: Machine Severity Stability**

This is the old agreement gate, reframed.

Examples:

- AC1 / Krippendorff across LLM judges,
- per-stratum disagreement rates,
- family-specific bias checks,
- deterministic guardrail override rates,
- consensus-vs-rule conflict rates.

If E2 fails, POLARIS can still report overall fabrication miss-rate, but it should not make strong severity-stratified claims.

**5. How Much Does This Weaken The Claim?**

A lot for clinical safety. Less for verifier engineering.

What remains strong:

> POLARIS can measure whether the verifier catches known, deterministically injected fabrications against cited sources.

That is a real benchmark. It is not fake.

What becomes weaker:

> POLARIS cannot claim that the benchmark’s severity labels are clinically expert-grounded.

It can say the severity strata are machine-adjudicated and provenance-constrained. That is useful, but it is not the same epistemic object.

Strongest defensible sentence:

> “POLARIS achieved a verifier miss-rate bounded at X% on a reproducible benchmark of deterministically injected fabrications against fetched cited sources; fabrication status labels are mechanical, while severity strata are provenance-constrained and cross-family-LLM adjudicated, not expert-validated.”

Even stronger if split:

> “The fabrication detection result is mechanically labeled. The severity-stratified result is machine-adjudicated and should be interpreted as a bench safety stress test, not clinical validation.”

That is the honest claim.

**7. Self-Deception Risks**

The biggest self-deception would be pretending LLM severity consensus equals clinical truth. It does not.

Other failure modes:

- **Benchmark over-templating**
  If mutations are too formulaic, the verifier may learn benchmark artifacts instead of real fabrication detection.

- **Source-selection bias**
  Structured sources are easier to validate than messy clinical prose. Performance may not transfer to unstructured papers, guidelines, or ambiguous real-world claims.

- **Severity laundering**
  A deterministic “dose field changed” flag does not automatically prove S0. It proves a safety-relevant field was altered. S0 still needs cautious wording unless the rule is explicitly defined as “S0-candidate.”

- **Citation-swap illusion**
  Replacing a citation does not always prove unsupportedness unless the mismatch is mechanically guaranteed.

- **Scope-overreach ambiguity**
  Some overreach is obvious; some requires domain judgment. Treating all of it as mechanical would be dishonest.

- **Judge contamination**
  If judge prompts, verifier prompts, and generator prompts share examples or rationales, agreement may reflect shared conditioning rather than independent validation.

- **Agreement worship**
  High AC1 among LLM judges can mean consistent bias. It is a stability signal, not a truth signal.

- **Clinical claim creep**
  The final report must not let “safety validation,” “clinical validation,” or “expert-grade” sneak back in through phrasing.

The hard line I would put into the contract:

> Human-free POLARIS validation is a reproducible bench test of verifier behavior under mechanically known fabrications. It is not clinical expert validation. Severity labels are machine-adjudicated risk strata and must be reported as such.
