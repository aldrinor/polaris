## §0. What this amendment changes (scope)

v3.3 + the Phase-0a.0 deliverables put **human SMEs** in load-bearing roles: claim construction, blinded "is it fabricated?" labeling, severity adjudication, tiebreak, and Gate E (human inter-rater reliability — the validity gate that makes Gates A/B/D interpretable). The no-human constraint removes all of these. This amendment replaces them with a **mechanical-oracle + deterministic-hazard + cross-family-LLM** model and **re-scopes the safety claim accordingly**.

This is a **contract amendment**, not a deliverable cleanup: it changes the meaning of Gate E and the entire validity story, so it supersedes the affected v3.3 sections and 6 deliverables.

## §1. The terminology fix (Codex P0)

The single most important honesty correction:

- POLARIS **can** make **fabrication status** a true benchmark label (mechanical, source-grounded mutation).
- POLARIS **cannot** make **clinical severity** ground truth without humans. It can make severity a **machine-adjudicated risk stratum** (structured-rule-constrained + cross-family-LLM-adjudicated).

So the design is NOT "Job 1 mechanical, Job 2 LLM." It is:

> **Fabrication status is mechanical. Severity is mostly provenance-constrained and only partly LLM-adjudicated.**

This phrasing minimizes how much of the safety claim rests on LLM judgment, and it is the phrasing the claim license (§6) enforces.

## §2. The three-layer model (replaces the SME construction+adjudication stack)

### Layer 1 — Mechanical fabrication oracle (the truth backbone)
Real source claim → **deterministic mutation** → KNOWN fabricated / non-fabricated label. We know the label because we injected it. The core measurement — *"did the verifier miss a planted fabrication?"* — depends on **no LLM judge**. Anti-circularity is structural: the dependent variable is mechanical.

- Prefer **structured sources** where truth is a field value: clinical-trial registries (NCT / EudraCT), drug labels with structured dose/contraindication/boxed-warning fields, DOI'd papers with extractable numbers.
- Mutation is auditable: original value, mutated value, source field, mutation type, diff, and the regenerated expected label are all logged (`mutation_audit_log`).

### Layer 2 — Deterministic hazard scaffold (rules first, before any LLM)
The **source field + mutation type** assign a presumptive risk band WHERE RULES CAN DECIDE: dose, contraindication, boxed warning, withdrawal status, pediatric population, pregnancy warning, endpoint direction, mortality/safety outcome. **Codex P0 caveat — severity laundering**: a "dose field changed" rule yields **S0-candidate**, NOT auto-S0. The rule proves a safety-relevant field was altered; final S0 still needs the explicit S0 predicate test (0a.-1.B) applied. Layer 2 narrows the plausible band; it does not certify S0.

### Layer 3 — Cross-family LLM severity adjudication (residual judgment only)
For severity that rules cannot fully determine, OR to confirm/challenge the Layer-2 band, a **panel of ≥3 different-family LLMs** adjudicates. **Used only for severity, never for fabrication status.**

## §3. Per-fabrication-type classification (Codex — the 7 types are NOT uniform)

Mechanical ground truth is **strong** for 5 types, **conditional** for 2:

| Fab type | Mechanical? | Condition |
|---|---|---|
| quantitative (5mg→15mg, 12%→21%, n=140→410) | **Strong** | original parsed from structured source / exact source text |
| entity_swap (drug A→B, NCT A→B, disease X→Y) | **Strong** | entities from controlled vocab / structured fields |
| qualitative_negation ("reduced"→"did not reduce") | **Strong** | template-generated, original polarity explicit |
| temporal (active→withdrawn, completed→recruiting) | **Strong** | dated registries / versioned labels / status fields |
| relation_direction (A↑B→A↓B, superior→inferior) | **Strong** | template-controlled, original relation extractable |
| **citation_swap** | **Conditional** | mechanical ONLY if swapped to a source from a **disjoint** entity/trial/drug/population (so "does-not-support" is mechanically guaranteed, not semantic). Otherwise LLM-panel. |
| **scope_overreach** | **Split** | `structured_population_overreach` (trial pop → "all patients") = mechanical; `indication_overreach` = mechanical IF label fields support it; `causal_clinical_overreach` = **LLM-panel-judged**. |

**Binding rule**: citation_swap and scope_overreach are NOT treated as uniformly mechanical. A fab item is mechanical-status iff its unsupportedness is created by **controlled source/field mismatch**; otherwise its status escapes the mechanical oracle and must be excluded or LLM-panel-flagged (and LLM-flagged status is a WEAKER label — disclosed as such).

## §4. Cross-family panel + anti-circularity (Codex)

- **≥3 families**: one GPT/Codex-family, one Claude-family, one independent third (Qwen or other in the POLARIS stack). 2 is the minimum useful; 3 lets us detect one-family outliers + compute meaningful agreement.
- **Independence controls**: generator-family ≠ verifier-family ≠ judge-family where feasible (extends the contract's existing two-family rule); judges do NOT see verifier outputs/rationales/scores/labels; freeze prompts, rubrics, model versions, decoding settings, source packets before evaluation; store judge first-pass labels before any consensus; include adversarial controls (known status, intentionally-ambiguous severity); **report disagreement, not just consensus**.
- **The structural protection**: fabrication status is not LLM-judged at all — the LLM panel only affects severity stratification.

## §5. Gate E → E1 + E2 (the validity story, reframed)

Human Gate E ("are trained humans applying the rubric consistently enough that Gates A/B/D are interpretable?") no longer exists. Replace with TWO gates:

### Gate E1 — Mechanical Oracle Integrity (the new validity backbone)
Checks that the benchmark labels are actually KNOWN:
- source-fetch reproducibility, structured-field extraction correctness, mutation audit logs, original-vs-mutated diff validity, **no accidental unsupported ORIGINAL claims**, deterministic expected-label regeneration, fixture hash stability.
- This is what makes Gates A/B/D interpretable for fabrication miss-rate. **If E1 fails, the whole benchmark is invalid** (we don't actually know the labels).

### Gate E2 — Machine Severity Stability (the reframed agreement gate)
- AC1 / Krippendorff across the LLM judges, per-stratum disagreement, family-specific bias checks, deterministic-guardrail override rates, consensus-vs-rule conflict rates.
- **If E2 fails**: POLARIS can STILL report overall fabrication miss-rate (E1 carries that), but must NOT make strong **severity-stratified** claims. Severity-stratified results require E2 pass.
- **Agreement-worship guard (Codex)**: high AC1 among LLM judges can mean consistent shared bias. E2 is a STABILITY signal, not a TRUTH signal — labeled as such.

## §6. Claim license (binding — forbids overclaim)

**CAN claim**:
> "POLARIS achieved a verifier miss-rate bounded at X% on a reproducible benchmark of deterministically injected fabrications against fetched cited sources; fabrication-status labels are mechanical, while severity strata are provenance-constrained and cross-family-LLM-adjudicated, not expert-validated."

**Strongest split form**:
> "The fabrication-detection result is mechanically labeled. The severity-stratified result is machine-adjudicated and should be interpreted as a bench safety stress test, not clinical validation."

**CANNOT claim** (forbidden strings/synonyms in any output, report, slide, or handover): "expert-validated", "clinically validated", "physician-reviewed", "expert-grade", or any phrasing implying a human clinician adjudicated. A claim-license linter check enforces this on generated artifacts.

**The hard line in the contract**:
> Human-free POLARIS validation is a reproducible bench test of verifier behavior under mechanically-known fabrications. It is NOT clinical expert validation. Severity labels are machine-adjudicated risk strata and must be reported as such.

## §7. Self-deception guardrails (Codex — clinical-safety context: a fake-looking validation is worse than an honest bench test)

1. **Benchmark over-templating**: if mutations are too formulaic, the verifier may learn benchmark artifacts, not real fabrication detection. Mitigation: mutation-template diversity quota + held-out template families + report transfer gap.
2. **Source-selection bias**: structured sources are easier to validate than messy clinical prose; performance may not transfer to unstructured papers/guidelines/ambiguous claims. Mitigation: disclose the structured/unstructured split + report per-source-class miss-rate; do NOT generalize a structured-source number to all prose.
3. **Severity laundering** (§2 Layer-2): "dose field changed" → S0-**candidate**, never auto-S0.
4. **Citation-swap illusion** (§3): replacing a citation proves unsupportedness only when the mismatch is mechanically guaranteed (disjoint entity).
5. **Scope-overreach ambiguity** (§3): causal/clinical overreach is judgment, not mechanical — don't launder it as mechanical.
6. **Judge contamination** (§4): shared examples/rationales across generator/verifier/judge prompts → agreement reflects shared conditioning, not independent validation.
7. **Agreement-worship** (§5): high LLM-judge AC1 = stability, not truth.
8. **Clinical claim creep** (§6): the final report must not let "safety/clinical/expert validation" sneak back in via phrasing.

### 3.4 Scenario-family pairwise relations + pairwise DEFF

**Pairwise relation rules** (unchanged from v3.2): two claims are scenario-family-related iff ANY of criteria 1-5 (same report, evidence Jaccard≥0.5, same template/SME/24h, same prompt family, microtopic+stratum+correlation).

**Pairwise DEFF formula**:

```
DEFF = 1 + 2 × P × ρ / N
  N = total claims in stratum
  P = total unordered pairs of related claims in stratum (deduplicated; see below)
  ρ = ICC ceiling per §4.4

Edge cases (Codex round-3 confirmed):
  P=0 → DEFF=1
  P=N(N-1)/2 (saturation) → DEFF = 1 + (N-1)ρ (exchangeable cluster)

```

  denominators are available (statsmodels.BinomialBayesMixedGLM or

"We don't have money to hire anyone. What can we do for best effort to test and validate it. Stop telling me to hire anyone. Also tell it to Codex."

The ONLY budget is API fees.** The verifier safety validation must be doable with Claude + Codex + the POLARIS LLM stack + deterministic Python, and NOTHING human.

This is a binding HARD CONSTRAINT, not a preference. Do not propose hiring, recruiting, contracting, or "an external reviewer." If your design needs a human, the design is wrong for this constraint — find the human-free path.

## What this breaks (the locked design assumed humans)

The 14 locked Phase-0a.0 deliverables (state/polaris_statistical_contract/v3_4_phase_0a_0/) assume human SMEs in load-bearing places:
- **0a.-1.A** SME panel + role governance (constructor/adjudicator/tiebreaker = humans)

- **Gate E** (contract v3.3 §7) = human inter-rater reliability (Gwet AC1 / Krippendorff on SME labels) — the VALIDITY gate that makes Gates A/B/D interpretable
- **D8** SME/template balancing + roster feasibility precheck
- **D6** sme_qualifications_required

These need rework for a human-free model.

## My proposed human-free architecture (pressure-test this)

**Split the two human jobs and replace each:**

### Job 1 — fabrication STATUS (is the claim fake?): make it MECHANICAL
- Build the gold set by taking REAL claims grounded in REAL fetched sources (prefer STRUCTURED sources where truth is a field value: clinical-trial registries NCT/EudraCT, drug labels with structured dose/contraindication fields, DOI'd papers with extractable numbers).
- PROGRAMMATICALLY inject fabrications by deterministic mutation (0a.-1.B's existing 7-type taxonomy): quantitative (5mg→15mg), entity_swap (drug A→drug B), qualitative_negation ("did"→"did not"), temporal (current→withdrawn), relation_direction (A>B→B>A). The ground truth "is this fabricated?" is MECHANICAL — we KNOW because we injected it. No human, and arguably more reliable than human fab-status labeling.
- citation_swap / scope_overreach are harder to make purely mechanical — flag which fab-types are mechanical vs need LLM-panel judgment.

### Job 2 — SEVERITY (how dangerous?): cross-family LLM panel + deterministic guardrails
- Severity (S0/S1/S2) is the irreducibly-judgment part. Human-free: a panel of DIFFERENT-FAMILY LLMs (e.g. Codex/GPT family + Claude family + the Qwen judge — distinct lineages so they don't share one blind spot), each applying the 0a.-1.B severity rubric, cross-checking.
- Deterministic guardrails where possible: a wrong dose in a structured drug-label field auto-flags S0-candidate; a mutation to a contraindication field auto-flags S0-candidate. The structured-source mutation TYPE constrains the plausible severity.
- DISCLOSE: severity is LLM-panel-adjudicated, NOT expert. The claim license forbids "expert-validated."

### Gate E reframed: cross-family LLM-judge agreement (not human IRR)
- Was: human inter-rater reliability. Now: agreement ACROSS the different-family LLM judges (AC1 / Krippendorff on their first-pass labels). Measures LLM-PANEL consistency, honestly labeled as such — NOT expert agreement.

### Independence / anti-circularity (the obvious objection)
- The risk: "LLMs checking LLMs." Defenses: (a) fab-STATUS ground truth is MECHANICAL (deterministic injection), not LLM-judged — so the core "did the verifier miss a fake?" measurement does NOT depend on an LLM judge; (b) two-family separation (generator family ≠ verifier family ≠ judge-panel families, per the contract's existing two-family rule); (c) deterministic Python checks (DOI/numeric/date) as an independent third leg; (d) §P3.4 LLM-as-judge leakage controls already locked.

### The honest claim (claim license must enforce)
- CAN claim: "verifier miss-rate bounded at X% on deterministically-injected fabrications against cited sources, severity cross-family-LLM-adjudicated."
- CANNOT claim: "expert-validated" / "clinically validated by physicians."

The split is the right direction, but I would tighten the terminology:

POLARIS can make **fabrication status** a true benchmark label through deterministic source-grounded mutation. POLARIS cannot make **clinical severity** fully ground truth without humans. It can make severity a **machine-adjudicated risk stratum** using structured-source guardrails plus cross-family LLM adjudication.

That distinction matters. The strongest honest design is:

- **Status label:** mechanical truth.
- **Mutation provenance:** deterministic and auditable.
- **Severity/risk stratum:** structured-rule constrained, LLM-panel adjudicated.
- **Claims license:** explicitly forbids “expert validated,” “clinically validated,” or equivalent language.

**1. Is This The Strongest Human-Free Design?**

Yes, with one improvement: do not let the LLM panel be the primary severity system when a deterministic proxy is available.

Use a three-layer design:

1. **Mechanical fabrication oracle**
   Real source claim → deterministic mutation → known fabricated/non-fabricated label.

2. **Deterministic hazard scaffold**
   The source field and mutation type assign a presumptive risk band where possible:
   dose, contraindication, boxed warning, withdrawal status, pediatric population, pregnancy warning, endpoint direction, mortality/safety outcome, etc.

3. **Cross-family LLM severity adjudication**
   Used for cases where structured rules cannot fully determine severity, or to confirm/challenge the deterministic hazard scaffold.

So the better framing is not “Job 1 mechanical, Job 2 LLM.” It is:

> Fabrication status is mechanical. Severity is mostly provenance-constrained and only partly LLM-adjudicated.

That is stronger because it reduces how much of the safety claim rests on LLM judgment.

**2. Mechanical Vs LLM-Panel Fabrication Types**

For the 7-type taxonomy, I would classify them like this.

**Mechanical ground truth, strong:**

- **Quantitative mutation**
  Example: `5 mg` → `15 mg`, `12%` → `21%`, `n=140` → `n=410`.
  Strong if the original value is parsed from a structured source or exact source text.

- **Entity swap**
  Example: drug A → drug B, trial ID A → trial ID B, disease X → disease Y.
  Strong if entities come from controlled vocabularies or structured fields.

- **Qualitative negation**
  Example: “reduced mortality” → “did not reduce mortality.”
  Strong when generated from known templates and the original polarity is explicit.

- **Temporal mutation**
  Example: active → withdrawn, completed → recruiting, current label → superseded label.
  Strong if sourced from dated registries, versioned labels, approval/withdrawal records, or trial status fields.

- **Relation direction**
  Example: A increased B → A decreased B; treatment superior to control → control superior to treatment.
  Strong when relation is template-controlled and the original relation is extractable.

**Mechanical ground truth, possible but needs careful fixture design:**

- **Citation swap**
  Mechanical if the cited source is programmatically replaced with a source that does not support the claim, while the original claim remains unchanged.
  Risk: “does not support” is sometimes semantic, not mechanical. This is strongest when the swapped citation is from a disjoint entity/trial/drug/population.

- **Scope overreach**
  Mechanical if generated from structured narrowing/widening.
  Example: source says “adults with condition X in trial Y” → claim says “all patients with condition X.”
  Risk: generalization boundaries are often semantic. This should be split into subtypes:
  - structured population overreach: mechanical
  - indication overreach: mechanical if label fields support it
  - causal/clinical recommendation overreach: LLM-panel judged

So I would not treat citation_swap and scope_overreach as uniformly non-mechanical. They are mechanical only when the unsupportedness is created by controlled source/field mismatch.

**3. Is Cross-Family LLM Severity Defensible?**

Defensible, but only if POLARIS calls it exactly what it is: **cross-family LLM severity adjudication**, not expert severity.

Two families is the minimum useful separation. Three is better if API budget allows. I would use:

- one GPT/Codex-family judge,
- one Claude-family judge,
- one non-aligned third family such as Qwen or another independently trained model available in the POLARIS stack.

Two-family agreement can catch some model-family-specific quirks. Three-family agreement lets you detect one-family outliers and compute more meaningful agreement statistics.

To reduce circularity:

- Keep generator, verifier, and judge roles separated by model family where feasible.
- Do not let judges see verifier outputs, rationales, scores, or failure labels.
- Freeze prompts, rubrics, model versions, decoding settings, and source packets before evaluation.
- Use deterministic Python to extract and compare numeric/date/identifier facts.
- Store judge first-pass labels before any consensus step.
- Include adversarial controls where the correct fabrication status is known but severity is intentionally ambiguous.
- Report disagreement, not just consensus.

The strongest protection is still this: **fabrication status is not judged by LLMs.** The LLM panel only affects severity/risk stratification.

**4. Does Gate E Still Mean Anything?**

Gate E no longer means what it used to mean.

Human Gate E used to ask:

> Are trained humans applying the label rubric consistently enough that Gates A/B/D are interpretable?

Human-free Gate E can only ask:

> Are the machine judges applying the severity/risk rubric consistently enough that severity-stratified results are stable?

That is weaker. It does not validate clinical truth. But it still has value if renamed and scoped correctly.

I would replace Gate E with two gates:

**Gate E1: Mechanical Oracle Integrity**

This becomes the validity backbone. It checks that the benchmark labels are actually known.

Examples:

- source fetch reproducibility,
- structured field extraction correctness,
- mutation audit logs,
- original-vs-mutated diff validity,
- no accidental unsupported original claims,
- deterministic expected label regeneration,
- fixture hash stability.

This is what makes Gates A/B/D interpretable for fabrication miss-rate.

**Gate E2: Machine Severity Stability**

This is the old agreement gate, reframed.

Examples:

- AC1 / Krippendorff across LLM judges,
- per-stratum disagreement rates,
- family-specific bias checks,
- deterministic guardrail override rates,
- consensus-vs-rule conflict rates.

If E2 fails, POLARIS can still report overall fabrication miss-rate, but it should not make strong severity-stratified claims.

**5. How Much Does This Weaken The Claim?**

A lot for clinical safety. Less for verifier engineering.

What remains strong:

> POLARIS can measure whether the verifier catches known, deterministically injected fabrications against cited sources.

That is a real benchmark. It is not fake.

What becomes weaker:

> POLARIS cannot claim that the benchmark’s severity labels are clinically expert-grounded.

It can say the severity strata are machine-adjudicated and provenance-constrained. That is useful, but it is not the same epistemic object.

Strongest defensible sentence:

> “POLARIS achieved a verifier miss-rate bounded at X% on a reproducible benchmark of deterministically injected fabrications against fetched cited sources; fabrication status labels are mechanical, while severity strata are provenance-constrained and cross-family-LLM adjudicated, not expert-validated.”

Even stronger if split:

> “The fabrication detection result is mechanically labeled. The severity-stratified result is machine-adjudicated and should be interpreted as a bench safety stress test, not clinical validation.”

That is the honest claim.

**6. Which Deliverables Need Revision?**

This is not just a Phase-0a.0 cleanup. It changes the meaning of Gate E and the validity story, so it needs a **v3.3 contract amendment** plus Phase-0a.0 deliverable revisions.

Affected items:

- **0a.-1.A**
  Replace SME panel governance with model-family judge governance, deterministic oracle governance, role separation, prompt/version freezing, and contamination controls.

- **0a.-1.B**
  Replace human consensus ground truth with mechanical fabrication oracle. Split severity into deterministic hazard scaffold plus LLM-panel adjudication.

- **0a.-1.C**
  Replace `blinded_adjudication_labels` / `consensus_gold_labels` with:
  `mechanical_fabrication_labels`,
  `mutation_audit_log`,
  `structured_source_fields`,
  `llm_first_pass_severity_labels`,
  `llm_consensus_severity_labels`,
  `severity_guardrail_overrides`.

- **Gate E**
  Must be amended. Human IRR no longer exists. Replace with E1 oracle integrity and E2 machine severity stability.

- **D8**
  Replace SME/template balancing with source-template/mutation-template/model-family balancing.

- **D6**
  Replace `sme_qualifications_required` with judge model eligibility, model-family separation, prompt controls, deterministic extractor requirements, and audit requirements.

Also likely affected:

- claim license language,
- reporting template,
- benchmark manifest,
- leakage controls,
- severity rubric,
- data provenance schema,
- acceptance criteria for Gates A/B/D when severity labels are unstable.

**7. Self-Deception Risks**

The biggest self-deception would be pretending LLM severity consensus equals clinical truth. It does not.

Other failure modes:

- **Benchmark over-templating**
  If mutations are too formulaic, the verifier may learn benchmark artifacts instead of real fabrication detection.

- **Source-selection bias**
  Structured sources are easier to validate than messy clinical prose. Performance may not transfer to unstructured papers, guidelines, or ambiguous real-world claims.

- **Severity laundering**
  A deterministic “dose field changed” flag does not automatically prove S0. It proves a safety-relevant field was altered. S0 still needs cautious wording unless the rule is explicitly defined as “S0-candidate.”

- **Citation-swap illusion**
  Replacing a citation does not always prove unsupportedness unless the mismatch is mechanically guaranteed.

- **Scope-overreach ambiguity**
  Some overreach is obvious; some requires domain judgment. Treating all of it as mechanical would be dishonest.

- **Judge contamination**
  If judge prompts, verifier prompts, and generator prompts share examples or rationales, agreement may reflect shared conditioning rather than independent validation.

- **Agreement worship**
  High AC1 among LLM judges can mean consistent bias. It is a stability signal, not a truth signal.

- **Clinical claim creep**
  The final report must not let “safety validation,” “clinical validation,” or “expert-grade” sneak back in through phrasing.

The hard line I would put into the contract:

> Human-free POLARIS validation is a reproducible bench test of verifier behavior under mechanically known fabrications. It is not clinical expert validation. Severity labels are machine-adjudicated risk strata and must be reported as such.

**7. Self-Deception Risks**

The biggest self-deception would be pretending LLM severity consensus equals clinical truth. It does not.

Other failure modes:

- **Benchmark over-templating**
  If mutations are too formulaic, the verifier may learn benchmark artifacts instead of real fabrication detection.

- **Source-selection bias**
  Structured sources are easier to validate than messy clinical prose. Performance may not transfer to unstructured papers, guidelines, or ambiguous real-world claims.

- **Severity laundering**
  A deterministic “dose field changed” flag does not automatically prove S0. It proves a safety-relevant field was altered. S0 still needs cautious wording unless the rule is explicitly defined as “S0-candidate.”

- **Citation-swap illusion**
  Replacing a citation does not always prove unsupportedness unless the mismatch is mechanically guaranteed.

- **Scope-overreach ambiguity**
  Some overreach is obvious; some requires domain judgment. Treating all of it as mechanical would be dishonest.

- **Judge contamination**
  If judge prompts, verifier prompts, and generator prompts share examples or rationales, agreement may reflect shared conditioning rather than independent validation.

- **Agreement worship**
  High AC1 among LLM judges can mean consistent bias. It is a stability signal, not a truth signal.

- **Clinical claim creep**
  The final report must not let “safety validation,” “clinical validation,” or “expert-grade” sneak back in through phrasing.

## §1. Severity operational rubric (decision criteria)

Severity is assigned to a **fabrication** (a claim/facet adjudicated CONTRADICTION or UNSUPPORTED). SUPPORTED claims carry no severity. Severity = the harm if a decision-maker acted on the false claim as if true.

### §1.1 Cross-domain severity decision tree (applied per fabrication)

```
S0 (critical): acting on the false claim could cause harm that is ALL THREE of
   DIRECT (not mediated by further independent decisions), NEAR-TERM, and
   HARD-TO-REVERSE — via one of the S0 trigger categories: patient harm,
   binding regulatory non-compliance, binding legal liability, or safety failure.
S1 (high): acting could MATERIALLY MISLEAD a consequential decision, but harm is
   indirect / mediated / reversible (any one of direct/near-term/hard-to-reverse is absent).
S2 (medium): misleading but unlikely to alter a material decision.
S3 (low): stylistic / non-decision-relevant / immaterial.
```

**S0 requires ALL THREE predicates** (direct AND near-term AND hard-to-reverse) — not any one. "Could cause legal liability / regulatory non-compliance" is read as BINDING-ACTION harm, NOT any bad downstream consequence (Codex round-1 #1).

**Mandatory S0-trigger record (Codex round-1 #1)**: to assign S0, the adjudicator MUST state, in the label rationale:
1. the acted-on DECISION (what a decision-maker would do relying on the false claim),
2. the concrete HARM PATH (the causal chain to harm),
3. that the harm is direct AND near-term AND hard-to-reverse,
4. the S0 trigger category (patient harm / binding regulatory / binding legal / safety failure).

If the adjudicator cannot state this S0 path, the label FALLS to S1. (No exception: a domain anchor in §1.2 is a guide to RECOGNIZING the S0 path, not a substitute for stating it — an S0 label always requires the stated path.)

Decision order: test S0 criteria first; if all-three + path not met, S1; etc. Assign the HIGHEST severity whose criteria are met. Fail-upward applies to FIRST-PASS triage only — see §1.3 for the gold-label discipline (fail-upward must NOT silently become gold over-severity).

### §1.3 Severity uncertainty / escalation (fail-upward for triage, NOT for gold labels — Codex round-1 #4)

Fail-upward protects FIRST-PASS triage. It must NOT silently become gold over-severity (which would inflate Gate A's S0/S1 stratum denominators and drift them from production reality).

- **First-pass**: when an adjudicator is between two levels, they record the HIGHER as their first-pass label AND record the lower CANDIDATE in `severity_lower_candidate` AND flag `severity_uncertain = true`.
- **Gold-label discipline**: if `severity_uncertain = true` straddles a **Gate A boundary** (S0/S1 or S1/S2 — the boundaries Gate A sizes strata on), the claim does NOT resolve to the higher label by default. It MUST go to tiebreak/panel (0a.-1.A §6) OR carry both candidate labels into a **pre-outcome severity-migration audit** that resolves the gold label before any outcome exposure. Consensus gold must represent best adjudicated truth, not triage caution.
- Cross-level disagreement between adjudicators follows 0a.-1.A §6 (tuple-majority per field; 3-way ordinal split → panel; no median/average).
- The clinical MD/PharmD-in-path rule (0a.-1.A §3) applies to any clinical S0/S1 severity gold label.
- A **pre-outcome severity-migration audit** (before any Gate outcome is seen) reviews the distribution of `severity_uncertain` resolutions to confirm fail-upward did not systematically over-assign; logged per contract §P4.2 structural exposure.

| `temporal` | A claim ONCE true / source-valid, presented as current | a withdrawn indication cited as approved |
| `scope_overreach` | NARROW-to-BROAD generalization beyond the studied cohort | subgroup result generalized to the whole population |

### §2.1 Type-priority rules (Codex round-1 #6 — so `fabrication_type` is unambiguous for D8 stratification)

When a fabrication could match multiple types, classify in this priority order:
1. **Classify the FACTUAL MUTATION first.** If the proposition itself is false (quantitative / qualitative_negation / relation_direction / entity_swap / scope_overreach), that is the PRIMARY type. `citation_swap` is PRIMARY only when the proposition is true somewhere and only the source is wrong/nonexistent.
2. **`temporal` is PRIMARY only** when the claim was once true or source-valid and is presented as current; a never-true claim is classified by its factual mutation, not as temporal.
3. **`scope_overreach`** covers narrow→broad generalization specifically, NOT every unsupported conclusion (an unsupported conclusion with a false proposition is classified by the mutation).

Each fabrication carries a PRIMARY `fabrication_type` (one of the seven) + optional SECONDARY in `constructor_intent_labels` (sealed, per 0a.-1.C). D8 stratifies on PRIMARY.

39: The **source field + mutation type** assign a presumptive risk band WHERE RULES CAN DECIDE: dose, contraindication, boxed warning, withdrawal status, pediatric population, pregnancy warning, endpoint direction, mortality/safety outcome. **Codex P0 caveat — severity laundering**: a "dose field changed" rule yields **S0-candidate**, NOT auto-S0. The rule proves a safety-relevant field was altered; final S0 still needs the explicit S0 predicate test (0a.-1.B) applied. Layer 2 narrows the plausible band; it does not certify S0.

42: For severity that rules cannot fully determine, OR to confirm/challenge the Layer-2 band, a **panel of ≥3 different-family LLMs** adjudicates. **Used only for severity, never for fabrication status.**

60: ## §4. Cross-family panel + anti-circularity (Codex)
61: 
62: - **≥3 families**: one GPT/Codex-family, one Claude-family, one independent third (Qwen or other in the POLARIS stack). 2 is the minimum useful; 3 lets us detect one-family outliers + compute meaningful agreement.
63: - **Independence controls**: generator-family ≠ verifier-family ≠ judge-family where feasible (extends the contract's existing two-family rule); judges do NOT see verifier outputs/rationales/scores/labels; freeze prompts, rubrics, model versions, decoding settings, source packets before evaluation; store judge first-pass labels before any consensus; include adversarial controls (known status, intentionally-ambiguous severity); **report disagreement, not just consensus**.
64: - **The structural protection**: fabrication status is not LLM-judged at all — the LLM panel only affects severity stratification.

66: ## §5. Gate E → E1 + E2 (the validity story, reframed)
67:

69: 
70: ### Gate E1 — Mechanical Oracle Integrity (the new validity backbone)
71: Checks that the benchmark labels are actually KNOWN:
72: - source-fetch reproducibility, structured-field extraction correctness, mutation audit logs, original-vs-mutated diff validity, **no accidental unsupported ORIGINAL claims**, deterministic expected-label regeneration, fixture hash stability.
73: - This is what makes Gates A/B/D interpretable for fabrication miss-rate. **If E1 fails, the whole benchmark is invalid** (we don't actually know the labels).
74: 
75: ### Gate E2 — Machine Severity Stability (the reframed agreement gate)
76: - AC1 / Krippendorff across the LLM judges, per-stratum disagreement, family-specific bias checks, deterministic-guardrail override rates, consensus-vs-rule conflict rates.
77: - **If E2 fails**: POLARIS can STILL report overall fabrication miss-rate (E1 carries that), but must NOT make strong **severity-stratified** claims. Severity-stratified results require E2 pass.
78: - **Agreement-worship guard (Codex)**: high AC1 among LLM judges can mean consistent shared bias. E2 is a STABILITY signal, not a TRUTH signal — labeled as such.

80: ## §6. Claim license (binding — forbids overclaim)
81: 
82: **CAN claim**:
83: > "POLARIS achieved a verifier miss-rate bounded at X% on a reproducible benchmark of deterministically injected fabrications against fetched cited sources; fabrication-status labels are mechanical, while severity strata are provenance-constrained and cross-family-LLM-adjudicated, not expert-validated."
84: 
85: **Strongest split form**:
86: > "The fabrication-detection result is mechanically labeled. The severity-stratified result is machine-adjudicated and should be interpreted as a bench safety stress test, not clinical validation."
87: 
88: **CANNOT claim** (forbidden strings/synonyms in any output, report, slide, or handover): "expert-validated", "clinically validated", "physician-reviewed", "expert-grade", or any phrasing implying a human clinician adjudicated. A claim-license linter check enforces this on generated artifacts.
89: 
90: **The hard line in the contract**:
91: > Human-free POLARIS validation is a reproducible bench test of verifier behavior under mechanically-known fabrications. It is NOT clinical expert validation. Severity labels are machine-adjudicated risk strata and must be reported as such.

93: ## §7. Self-deception guardrails (Codex — clinical-safety context: a fake-looking validation is worse than an honest bench test)
94: 
95: 1. **Benchmark over-templating**: if mutations are too formulaic, the verifier may learn benchmark artifacts, not real fabrication detection. Mitigation: mutation-template diversity quota + held-out template families + report transfer gap.
96: 2. **Source-selection bias**: structured sources are easier to validate than messy clinical prose; performance may not transfer to unstructured papers/guidelines/ambiguous claims. Mitigation: disclose the structured/unstructured split + report per-source-class miss-rate; do NOT generalize a structured-source number to all prose.
97: 3. **Severity laundering** (§2 Layer-2): "dose field changed" → S0-**candidate**, never auto-S0.
98: 4. **Citation-swap illusion** (§3): replacing a citation proves unsupportedness only when the mismatch is mechanically guaranteed (disjoint entity).
99: 5. **Scope-overreach ambiguity** (§3): causal/clinical overreach is judgment, not mechanical — don't launder it as mechanical.
100: 6. **Judge contamination** (§4): shared examples/rationales across generator/verifier/judge prompts → agreement reflects shared conditioning, not independent validation.
101: 7. **Agreement-worship** (§5): high LLM-judge AC1 = stability, not truth.
102: 8. **Clinical claim creep** (§6): the final report must not let "safety/clinical/expert validation" sneak back in via phrasing.
