The operator directive is to **replace the forewarning/fringe framing** with a **neutral multi-perspective presentation where competing world-views are shown as legitimate, each carrying its real (possibly asymmetric) evidence weight, and the user judges** — no bias, no stamps, no forewarning prose.\n\n### 2.2 The hard tension, named and resolved (do not skip this)\n\nThere is a real conflict between two binding constraints, and both sides have a lethal failure mode:\n\n- The operator wants **no fringe/forewarning stamp, all sides legitimate, no bias.**\n- The **clinical-safety invariant #5** (binding, preserved) plus Dixon-Clarke 2013 (PubMed 23193194, measured harm: falsely-balanced vaccine reporting *reduced* perceived safety and vaccination intent), Wikipedia FRINGE, and §-1.1 all say **equal treatment of unequal evidence is itself bias** and is "lethal in clinical." I am not authorized to drop invariant #5.\n\n**The reconciliation — handed to us nearly verbatim by all three streams: replace the LABEL with the BASIS.** Wikipedia NPOV's "neutral *editing*, not neutral *content*"; GRADE's "show the basis, let the user infer"; the no-warning rule from stream #2 is satisfiable **if and only if the criteria behind any weight are displayed**. So:\n\n- The **system** stamps nothing — no "fringe," no "debunked," no forewarning sentence. That is the neutrality.\n- The **evidence weight** carries the (often asymmetric) truth, rendered as a visible **per-perspective evidence-basis panel** the user reads and judges. The asymmetry — *14 RCTs + 3 systematic reviews [High]* vs *2 case reports + mechanistic argument [Very Low]* — becomes **visible in the artifact** instead of **asserted in a stamp**. The user infers; the system does not editorialize.\n- "Legitimate" means **shown with its real (possibly low) weight and one-click drill-down to span + verdict** — NOT "shown as equally supported." A genuine world-view dispute with no empirical consensus (values, forecasting) shows steelmanned sides at honest parity; an empirical dispute with consensus shows the real weight distribution.\n- The medical-misinfo case is handled **without the banned word**: its panel is simply **near-empty of independent high-tier basis**, and where there is no independent consensus the claim **ABSTAINs** ("contested; no independent consensus" — reusing existing refuse/unresolved behavior). This preserves invariant #5's *substance* (not normalized as an equal side) while dropping the stamp.\n\nThis is neutral AND anti-false-balance AND §-1.1-safe simultaneously — the operator's "let the user judge" satisfied by *showing the basis*, not by *equalizing the boxes*.\n\n### 2.3 The exact composition mechanism\n\nPer question, the composition path first **diagnoses the contestation type** (this IS the adaptive guardrail, copied from Anthropic's even-handedness "lack-of-consensus" discriminator + OpenAI's seek-the-truth-together):\n\n- **empirical-with-consensus** (vaccines, climate) → due-weight: each side shown, weight proportional to verified-evidence quality; the asymmetry is the message, carried by the basis panel.\n- **genuine-uncertainty** (open scientific questions) → separate *supported* from *open*; sides at honest parity.\n- **values dispute** (politics, ethics) → **steelman each legitimate view** (Ideological Turing Test, neutral terminology, no verdict); no evidence-weight asymmetry asserted because there is no due weight to defer to.\n- **forecasting** (AI-and-jobs) → scenario spread, projective not factual.\n\nEach perspective is gathered through its **own retrieval lens** (MODS-style per-perspective retrieval + citation, arXiv 2502.00322 — raw LLMs silently drop minority views; per-perspective retrieval prevents the drop), then composed via the **existing 4-role / voter-arbiter agreement** (cross-lineage, open-weight voters — the bridging/balanced-panel analog) — **not a new adjudication path**. Reranking-for-balance (arXiv 2506.15925) selects the most balanced faithful candidate, because direct just-prompt-balanced generation empirically skews. **The weight attached to each perspective is evidence QUALITY × independent-origin count, never document count** — else we rebuild Ground News's source-count bias inside the summarizer.\n\n### 2.4 The friendly UI artifact — design\n\nBuilt on the **components that already exist** (#744 citation chip + hover source card, #745 per-claim verdict chip, #747 Proof Replay split-view, #757 report page, #758 claim-anchored compare view) — not designed from scratch.\n\n**The artifact: a claim-anchored "Perspectives" panel** that opens from any contested sentence (reusing the compare-view host), containing per legitimate perspective:\n\n1. **A neutral position header** — "Position held by [who], with this evidence behind it" — no "fringe"/"warning"/"debunked". A steelmanned one-line statement of the view in neutral terms.\n2. **An evidence-basis sub-panel (GRADE-style, the load-bearing element):** the study types / source tiers / independent-origin count behind that perspective, rendered as **frequencies, not a percentage or a lone dot** — "*8 of n: RCTs + systematic reviews [High certainty]*" vs "*2 of n: case reports + mechanistic argument [Very Low]*". The certainty label is acceptable **only because its downgrade criteria are shown** (study design / consistency / directness / independence). This makes the asymmetry self-evident without a stamp.\n3. **Scite-style polarity badges** (supporting / contrasting / contextualizing) on each cited source — **neutral hue + icon + text label, never color alone** (colorblind safety; red=contrasting reads as red=wrong and biases toward consensus — explicitly avoided).\n4. **Drill-down:** every badge, every basis count, every panel is **one click from the raw `[#ev]` span and its VERIFIED/PARTIAL verdict** — the Scite "self-correcting" property: a mis-classified badge is exposed by the citing sentence sitting right behind it, and a meter without drill-down would violate the §-1.1 metadata-audit ban.\n\n**Panel sizing defeats false balance structurally, not with a caveat:** panels are **sized/annotated by real verified-evidence weight**, not equal-width — because equal real estate reads as equal credibility (the false-balance trap), and a caveat is empirically insufficient (Effectiviology / false-balance literature). The asymmetry is *in the layout and the basis panel*, not asserted in prose.\n\n**Three named anti-patterns explicitly rejected** (all from the streams, all as negative templates):\n- **Ground News source-count / coverage bar** — measures who-is-saying-this (volume), not evidence weight; §-1.1 bans source counts as quality signals. If a composition strip is shown at all, its axis is **evidence tier / source type**, never ideology or headcount, and it is kept visually separate from the weight panel so "many sources" never reads as "true."\n- **Bare confidence/credibility score (% or traffic-light dot)** — the best-documented HARM pattern: overconfident displays (80% shown / 70% accurate) pushed AI-advice adoption 57%→69.6%, 64% of users mis-judged calibration, and in a 2025 breast-cancer CDSS high confidence *reduced* diagnostic accuracy via overreliance. POLARIS's honest replacement is the per-sentence VERIFIED/PARTIAL verdict against a real span — never a glanceable dot.\n- **Symmetric 50/50 equal-box presentation** — manufactures false balance; the Dixon-Clarke measured harm.\n\n### 2.5 The honest gap + exactly what POLARIS builds for L6\n\n**HAVE:** the disclosure substrate (per-sentence `[#ev]` tokens, `strict_verify` verdict ladder, `SentenceVerification` additive fields), the existing 4-role/voter-arbiter agreement, independence-collapse for origin-count, and the full UI component kit (citation chip, verdict chip, Proof Replay, compare view).\n**MISSING:** the contestation-type classifier, per-perspective retrieval + composition that defeats false balance by weight, and the Perspectives artifact rendering basis panels.\n**BUILD (revision to Phase 7):**\n1.
