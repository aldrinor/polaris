# Research report: What is the efficacy and safety of semaglutide 2.4mg for weight loss in adults with obesity?

In the STEP 1 randomized controlled trial, semaglutide 2.4 mg achieved a mean weight loss of 14.9% at week 68.[1] The STEP 3 trial combining semaglutide 2.4 mg with intensive behavioral therapy produced a mean weight loss of 16.0% at week 68.[2] Over 104 weeks of continued therapy, the STEP 5 extension trial reported mean weight loss of 17.4%.[3] A network meta-analysis ranked semaglutide first with a pooled estimate of 15.4% reduction.[4] On the safety axis, nausea was the most common adverse event, occurring in 44.2% of semaglutide recipients.[5] Treatment discontinuation due to adverse events was 3.4% in the STEP 3 semaglutide arm.[6]
