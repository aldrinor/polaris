Semaglutide 2.4 mg produces significant weight loss in adults with obesity, with one trial showing 86.6% of participants achieving at least 5% body-weight loss at week 68 compared to 47.6% with placebo [ev_001]. The magnitude of weight loss is substantial, with semaglutide-treated patients losing 14.9% of baseline weight compared to 2.4% with placebo over 68 weeks, a difference of -12.4 percentage points [ev_003], and it produces approximately 15% of initial weight loss, the largest of any obesity medication to date [ev_002]. Participants are 2.37 times as likely to achieve ≥5% weight loss and 15.08 times as likely to achieve ≥20% weight loss with semaglutide [ev_006][ev_007]. Beyond weight loss, semaglutide improves waist circumference, fasting plasma glucose, and lipid profiles compared to placebo [ev_014] and is FDA-approved to reduce the risk of major adverse cardiovascular events in adults with established cardiovascular disease and obesity or overweight [ev_019]. The most common adverse events are gastrointestinal, such as nausea and diarrhea, which are generally mild-to-moderate and transient [ev_005]. One real-world analysis noted that tirzepatide was associated with a lower risk of cardiovascular events, especially incident heart failure, compared with semaglutide [ev_010].
