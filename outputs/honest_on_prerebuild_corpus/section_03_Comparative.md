# Comparative

Focus: This section evaluates semaglutide's performance relative to other obesity medications and placebo across different populations.

## Raw draft
In a Canadian cost-effectiveness analysis, semaglutide 2.4 mg was evaluated alongside other pharmacotherapies including orlistat, naltrexone/bupropion, and liraglutide 3.0 mg [ev_000]. A separate real-world analysis reported that tirzepatide was associated with a lower risk of cardiovascular events, especially incident heart failure, compared with semaglutide in non-diabetic adults with obesity [ev_010]. For context on placebo response, in the STEP 2 trial the mean weight loss with placebo was 3.4% [ev_018].
