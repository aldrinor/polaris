# Efficacy

Focus: Analyze the magnitude of weight loss achieved with semaglutide treatment compared to placebo and established thresholds.

## Raw draft
Semaglutide treatment resulted in a significantly higher proportion of participants achieving clinically meaningful weight-loss thresholds compared to placebo, with 86.6% of semaglutide participants versus 47.6% of placebo achieving at least 5% body-weight loss at week 68 [ev_001]. Semaglutide participants were 2.37 times as likely to achieve ≥5% weight loss and 15.08 times as likely to achieve ≥20% weight loss than those on placebo [ev_006][ev_007]. Higher proportions of patients on semaglutide achieved weight loss exceeding 5%, 10%, 15%, and 20% [ev_012][ev_015]. In terms of percentage weight change, one review describes semaglutide as producing the largest weight loss of any obesity medication to date with reductions of approximately 15% of initial weight at 68 weeks [ev_002]. Specific trial data shows semaglutide-treated patients lost 14.9% of baseline weight compared with 2.4% for placebo over 68 weeks, a treatment difference of −12.4 percentage points [ev_003]. Another source reports patients given semaglutide lost an average of 15% of their body weight over a 16-month period compared with a 2.4% loss for those given placebo [ev_009]. A meta-analysis found semaglutide 2.4 mg reduced body weight by 8.20% more than placebo [ev_013]. Furthermore, semaglutide 2.4 mg was associated with a greater percentage weight change from baseline versus all available comparators at 52 weeks [ev_004].
