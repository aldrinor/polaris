Semaglutide 2.4 mg produces significant weight loss in adults with obesity, with one trial showing 86.6% of participants achieving at least 5% body-weight loss at week 68 compared to 47.6% with placebo [#ev:ev_001:0-34]. The magnitude of weight loss is substantial, with semaglutide-treated patients losing 14.9% of baseline weight compared to 2.4% with placebo over 68 weeks, a difference of -12.4 percentage points [#ev:ev_003:4-68], and it produces approximately 15% of initial weight loss, the largest of any obesity medication to date . Participants are 2.37 times as likely to achieve ≥5% weight loss and 15.08 times as likely to achieve ≥20% weight loss with semaglutide [#ev:ev_006:0-42][#ev:ev_007:0-49]. Beyond weight loss, semaglutide improves waist circumference, fasting plasma glucose, and lipid profiles compared to placebo [#ev:ev_014:0-83] and is FDA-approved to reduce the risk of major adverse cardiovascular events in adults with established cardiovascular disease and obesity or overweight [#ev:ev_019:0-115]. The most common adverse events are gastrointestinal, such as nausea and diarrhea, which are generally mild-to-moderate and transient [#ev:ev_005:0-144]. One real-world analysis noted that tirzepatide was associated with a lower risk of cardiovascular events, especially incident heart failure, compared with semaglutide [#ev:ev_010:0-151].
