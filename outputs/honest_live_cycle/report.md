# Research report: What is the efficacy and safety of semaglutide 2.4mg for weight loss in adults with obesity?

In the STEP 3 trial, when added to intensive behavioral therapy, semaglutide 2.4 mg led to an average body weight loss of 16.0% from baseline after 68 weeks.[1] More participants achieved weight loss of at least 5% at week 104 with semaglutide (77.1%) than with placebo (34.4%).[2] A post hoc analysis of STEP trials 1-3 and 5 found that efficacy was maintained in participants using antidepressants at baseline, with a mean weight change of -15.7% for semaglutide versus -0.2% for placebo in STEP 1 for that subgroup.[3] In the STEP UP trial, which included a 7.2 mg comparator, the 2.4 mg dose resulted in a weight loss of 17.5% based on the trial product estimand after 72 weeks.[4] Regarding safety, gastrointestinal adverse events were more common with semaglutide than placebo, with 82.2% versus 53.9% reporting them in STEP 5, and these events were mostly mild-to-moderate.[2] The prevalence of adverse events was generally similar between semaglutide and placebo in participants on or off antidepressants.[3]
