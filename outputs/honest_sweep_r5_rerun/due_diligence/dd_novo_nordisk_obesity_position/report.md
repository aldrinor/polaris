### Efficacy

The company's strategic expansion is partly driven by the need to scale production, with its parent company, Novo Holdings A/S, acquiring Catalent for $16.5 billion in February 2024 to allow Novo Nordisk to purchase three manufacturing facilities for $11 billion, a move crucial for scaling production of its key drugs.[5] One source notes that regulatory scrutiny shapes how Novo Nordisk defends its market share.[6] The company's market access is supported by expanding into new indications and achieving access wins in international markets.[6]

### Long-term Outcomes

This growth is underpinned by a large potential patient population, as the global population affected by overweight and obesity is estimated to increase from 2.6 billion in 2020 to over four billion by 2035.[7] However, recent performance indicates mounting pressure, as Novo Nordisk has cut its full-year outlook for the fourth time in 2025, citing slowing demand for its GLP-1 treatments and increased competition.[8] Wegovy sales, while setting a quarterly record at around 21 billion Danish kroner in Q4, rose only 18% year-over-year in the third quarter to DK20.35 billion, which was below expectations.[8] Analysts have warned that clinical setbacks could curb long-term sales hopes and leave Novo struggling to win back market share in the fast-growing obesity treatment market, where demand is increasingly driven by drugs offering the greatest weight-loss results.[9]
