# Research report: What are current clinical guidelines for oral anticoagulation in adults with non-valvular atrial fibrillation?

### Efficacy

Oral anticoagulants are effective at reducing harm and mortality from thromboembolic events, such as stroke, in patients with atrial fibrillation.[1] For most patients with non-valvular atrial fibrillation, treatment with a direct-acting oral anticoagulant (DOAC) is appropriate, with currently available options being apixaban, rivaroxaban, and dabigatran.[1] One source reports that emulation of the ARISTOTLE and ROCKET AF trials in real-world atrial fibrillation patients results in similar efficacy and safety as the original landmark trials.[1] In contrast, warfarin remains the first-line oral anticoagulant for valvular AF in specific patient subgroups, such as those with mechanical heart valves or rheumatic mitral stenosis.[1]
