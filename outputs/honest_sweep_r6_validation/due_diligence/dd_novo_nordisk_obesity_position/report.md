# Research report: What is Novo Nordisk's competitive position in the obesity pharmaceutical market relative to Eli Lilly and newer entrants?

### Comparative

Eli Lilly and Novo Nordisk are the two leaders in the obesity drug market, though over the past year both have lagged broader equities, with Novo Nordisk's stock performing substantially worse than Eli Lilly's.[1][2] Eli Lilly's pipeline includes retatrutide, which in a phase 3 study led to a 28.7% mean weight loss after 68 weeks, a performance no other drug has come close to.[1][2] Novo Nordisk is seeking to turn the tide with approvals for an oral version of Wegovy and a high-dose formulation, as well as pipeline candidates like CagriSema.[1] One source states Eli Lilly's weight loss portfolio seems much stronger than Novo Nordisk's, while another concludes Novo's ability to deliver strong efficacy and consistent availability positions it ahead of Eli Lilly in the obesity treatment market.[1][3] Despite the valuation gap, one analysis concludes Eli Lilly remains the more attractive stock, having earned a premium for its breakthroughs.[1][2]

### Regulatory

However, the company also faced a significant clinical setback, described by analysts as a trial miss that could curb demand for its next-generation drug CagriSema and temper long-term sales hopes.[4] This setback adds to investor worries about intensifying competition in a market where demand is increasingly driven by drugs offering the greatest weight-loss results.[4] The company's declining U.S. market share and expectations for lower pharmaceutical exports have been cited as factors impacting even Denmark's economic growth forecast.[5]

### Population Subgroups

The oral version of Wegovy reportedly saw robust initial demand and strong early prescription trends in the U.S. just one week after its early-January launch, signaling encouraging early uptake.[6] The weight management indication for obesity and overweight is expected to hold the largest market share, at 57.8% in 2026.[7] By age group, the adults’ segment is expected to dominate the global Wegovy market, contributing 68.1% in 2026.[7] This growth is driven by a large and expanding patient base, as the World Obesity Federation estimates the global population with obesity or overweight will increase from 2.6 billion individuals in 2020 to over four billion by 2035.[8]

### Long-term Outcomes

The market is also being shaped by new entrants and technological advancements, which demand continuous innovation from the company.[9] However, the company is proactively defending its position through massive capital investment, committing tens of billions to expand production capacity  and through strategic acquisitions like Catalent to secure manufacturing scale.[10][9] The long-term sustainability of its dominance will depend on navigating these dynamics within a rapidly expanding global GLP-1 market, which is projected to reach USD 324.5 billion by 2035.[9]
