# Research report: What are current clinical guidelines for oral anticoagulation in adults with non-valvular atrial fibrillation?

### Regulatory

For non-valvular atrial fibrillation (AF), direct-acting oral anticoagulants (DOACs) are preferred over warfarin in many guidelines.[1] The currently available DOACs are apixaban, rivaroxaban, and dabigatran.[2] Compared to warfarin, DOACs have demonstrated superiority with regard to prevention of stroke and systemic embolism, with dabigatran 150 mg twice daily showing superiority in stroke prevention.[1]

### Limitations

Limitations: The corpus is heavily skewed toward lower-tier sources, with only 45% of sources classified as T1 primary studies. The remaining 55% of evidence comes from T4 and T7 sources, which represent secondary syntheses and commentaries, respectively. This tier-distribution gap may introduce interpretive bias, as the foundational primary evidence constitutes less than half of the analyzed material. Furthermore, the evidence horizon spans from 2010 to the present, potentially omitting key foundational research published prior to this 14-year window.
