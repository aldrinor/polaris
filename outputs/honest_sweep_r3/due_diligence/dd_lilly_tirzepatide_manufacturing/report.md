# Research report: What is the current state of Eli Lilly's tirzepatide manufacturing capacity and supply outlook?

### Regulatory

The market for tirzepatide is experiencing explosive demand, with the combined franchise generating $10.1 billion in a single quarter and becoming the world's best-selling drug.[1] Regulatory and coverage dynamics are pivotal; in the U.S., insurers are increasingly covering GLP-1s for diabetes, while coverage for obesity drugs is more patchy but improving.[2] However, high costs remain a major barrier, with weight-loss GLP-1s accounting for an average of 10.5% of total annual claims among employers in 2025.[3]

### Dose Response

This includes a specific $9 billion investment at its LEAP Lebanon Innovation and Research District in Indiana, which more than doubled an initial $3.7 billion commitment and is described by the company as the single largest investment in synthetic medicine API manufacturing in U.S. history.[4][5] Lilly has also invested $3 billion to expand the injectable manufacturing facility it acquired in Pleasant Prairie, Wisconsin, calling this its single largest U.S. manufacturing investment outside Indiana.[6][7] One source reports the total manufacturing commitments since 2020 exceed $18 billion, while another states the total is more than $23 billion.[8][5][6][7] These investments are aimed at boosting production of tirzepatide to meet soaring demand, though certain dose strengths of Zepbound and Mounjaro have been listed in shortage.[6][5]

### Comparative

In response, Eli Lilly has launched a multi-state, multi-year U.S. manufacturing build-out specifically to boost output for tirzepatide and other medicines.[9] The company is also accelerating scale-up with a separate $4.5 billion "Medicine Foundry" on the same campus.[9]
