# Research report: What is the current state of Eli Lilly's tirzepatide manufacturing capacity and supply outlook?

### Dose Response

Since 2020, Eli Lilly has committed more than $16 billion to develop new manufacturing sites in the U.S. and Europe, with total manufacturing investments exceeding $18 billion.[1] This Indiana expansion, which includes an additional $5.3 billion commitment announced in 2024 to more than double a prior $3.7 billion investment, is specifically intended to manufacture tirzepatide to meet demand for Zepbound and Mounjaro, with production expected to start towards the end of 2026 and scale up through 2028.[2][3] Separately, Lilly invested $3 billion to expand a Wisconsin injectable manufacturing facility it acquired from Nexus Pharmaceuticals, an investment the company described as its "single largest U.S. manufacturing investment outside our home state of Indiana".[4][5]

### Population Subgroups

This surge is fueled by a significant increase in new patient starts and rapid market share capture in both type 2 diabetes and obesity treatment.[6] This strategy is enabled by the resolution of FDA-declared tirzepatide shortages and the launch of six new manufacturing facilities to meet surging demand.[7] The overall GLP-1 market, which includes tirzepatide, is expected to grow significantly in 2026 due to factors like reduced prices and expanded access.[8] Eli Lilly has raised its financial forecasts, now expecting full-year revenue of between $63 billion and $63.5 billion for 2025, up from a previous estimate.[9]

### Long-term Outcomes

The company has also added a $4.5 billion "Medicine Foundry" in Indiana to speed development and scale-up, with production acceleration targeted later in the decade.[10] This strategic rewiring of manufacturing capacity is part of the firm's focus on ramping up supply alongside developing more convenient pills.[11]
