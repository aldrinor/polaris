# Research report: How is the FDA regulating AI-enabled medical devices under the current Predetermined Change Control Plan framework?

### Regulatory

The FDA's Predetermined Change Control Plan (PCCP) framework is a regulatory pathway designed to support iterative improvement of AI-enabled medical devices while providing a reasonable assurance of safety and effectiveness.[1][2] The FDA, Health Canada, and the UK's MHRA have jointly identified five guiding principles for PCCPs, which draw upon broader Good Machine Learning Practice principles, including monitoring deployed models for performance.[3][4][5] If the FDA authorizes a PCCP, the manufacturer can implement the described modifications without needing new marketing submissions for each change.[6][7][8] This approach addresses the regulatory burden posed by traditional change control, which requires new submissions for significant software modifications, a process ill-suited for continuously learning AI.[7][9] The final guidance for AI-enabled device software functions was published in December 2024, formalizing this pathway.[6] The PCCP framework is intended to ensure the continued safety and effectiveness of a device throughout its lifecycle without necessitating additional marketing submissions for each pre-approved modification.[1][10][2]

### Mechanism

The FDA's AI/ML Action Plan outlines a mechanism for oversight that includes issuing draft guidance on a predetermined change control plan for software's learning over time.[11] The complex and dynamic processes in the development, deployment, use, and maintenance of AI technologies are noted to benefit from careful management throughout the medical product life cycle.[12][13] The FDA encourages the development of innovative, safe, and effective medical devices that incorporate artificial intelligence.[14] The action plan is intended to advance the Agency's oversight and is anticipated to continue to evolve over time.[11]
