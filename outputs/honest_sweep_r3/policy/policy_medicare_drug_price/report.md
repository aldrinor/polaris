# Research report: What is the impact of Medicare drug-price negotiation under the Inflation Reduction Act on drug list prices and access?

### Regulatory

For the third cycle of the program, 15 drugs covered under Medicare Part B or Part D were selected for negotiation in 2026, with negotiated prices taking effect on January 1, 2028.[1][2] These selected drugs accounted for total gross Medicare spending of $27 billion between November 2024 and October 2025, during which 1.8 million Medicare beneficiaries used them.[1][2] In the prior second cycle, 15 additional Part D drugs were selected for negotiation in 2025, with prices effective in 2027.[3] Drugs selected for negotiation are chosen from the top-ranking qualifying negotiation eligible drugs based on total Medicare spending.[1]

### Efficacy

For beneficiaries, people with Medicare Part D prescription drug coverage are projected to save an estimated $1.5 billion in out-of-pocket costs in 2026 when the prices take effect.[4][5] For Medicare Part B drugs, the median annual WAC growth rose more sharply, from 6.5% pre-policy to 13.3% post-policy, roughly doubling the pre-policy pace.[6]

### Comparative

CMS also estimated that Medicare beneficiaries will save $1.5 billion when the negotiated prices for the first set of drugs take effect in 2026.[7] The file containing the negotiated prices is updated annually to show the inflation-adjusted negotiated price for each selected drug.[8] However, a provision delaying the negotiation of certain orphan drugs like Keytruda and Opdivo is estimated to cost the federal government $8.8 billion over a decade and lead to higher out-of-pocket costs for beneficiaries.[7]

### Limitations
