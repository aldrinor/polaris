# Research report: What is the impact of Medicare drug-price negotiation under the Inflation Reduction Act on drug list prices and access?

### Regulatory

Drugs are selected from the top-ranking qualifying negotiation eligible drugs based on total Medicare spending.[1] The selection of the biologic drugs Keytruda and Opdivo, on which Medicare and beneficiaries spent $5.6 billion and $2.0 billion respectively in 2023, was delayed beyond 2026 due to a change in law affecting orphan drug provisions.[1]

### Efficacy

Early analysis of the Medicare Drug Price Negotiation Program shows its initial impact on list prices has been mixed, with acceleration observed in some drug categories prior to negotiated prices taking effect.[2] This acceleration was broad-based, with products that had a medium starting inflation tier (6-10%) showing the largest increase, rising from 6.9% to 14.1%.[2] The Part D data shows a compression pattern where products with lower pre-policy growth accelerated the most, while the highest-growth group slowed from 17.0% to 10.5%.[2] Among specific drugs selected for negotiation, pricing behavior varied, with some like etanercept and rivaroxaban seeing their rate of price increase accelerate by 6.0 percentage points post-policy, while others like ibrutinib and sacubitril/valsartan saw slower increases.[2]
