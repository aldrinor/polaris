## 1. THE BROAD DR BAR — what an analyst-grade broad report actually requires

Read from `state/compare_chatgpt_q1.md`, `compare_gemini_q1..q5.md`, and citation behavior traced through `compare_gemini_dr.txt` / `compare_chatgpt_dr.txt`. Six concrete requirements, each with a quoted exemplar.

**1.1 A QUESTION-SHAPED section model, not a fixed skeleton.** ChatGPT q1 is a 9-section analyst artifact: `Executive summary → Market baseline + comparison table → Cost/TCO → Performance/scalability → Jurisdiction/compliance/security → Risk matrix + recommended architecture → Assumptions, sources, gaps` (`compare_chatgpt_q1.md:9,21,34,75,85,99,137`). q2 (CUSMA) invents an entirely different outline: `Legal architecture of the Article 34.7 review → Tariff environment → Canada's retaliation matrix → Strategic leverage points → Path to July 2026` (`compare_gemini_q2.md:66,86,106,166,281`). The outline is DERIVED FROM the question; there is no universal "Efficacy/Safety" skeleton.

**1.2 A QUANTIFIED trade-off with explicit break-evens + sensitivity.** The single biggest differentiator. ChatGPT q1: *"the model beats AWS on-demand H100 pricing once utilization is above roughly 32%, beats GCP DWS-like reserved pricing at roughly 40%... utilization matters far more than electricity price"* (`compare_chatgpt_q1.md:38`), backed by a TCO model — *"a 512-GPU sovereign core in this model is about US$61 million over 5 years, versus approximately US$123 million for 512 H100 GPUs kept busy at 80% utilization on AWS on-demand"* (`:73`) — and a 6-row sensitivity table (`:64-71`).

**1.3 A DECISION ARTIFACT (comparison table / risk matrix / conditional recommendation), not a verdict.** q5 builds a 5-column institutional-comparison matrix across Quebec RPAM / NZ PHARMAC / UK NHS-VPAG (`compare_gemini_q5.md:207-213`) where each cell is a defended claim ("Cost Containment: Poor (75% higher spend) / Aggressive (21-79% lower prices) / Moderate-Strong"). Gemini q1's best move is a bifurcated recommendation by data class: *"For Protected B AI workloads... heavily favors leveraging the mature, localized hyperscaler ecosystems"* vs *"For Protected C... demands absolute reliance on true domestic sovereign infrastructure"* (`compare_gemini_q1.md:7`).

**1.4 REAL broad-policy source reach + correct tiering for it.** The load-bearing sources are government/regulator/statistical-agency/think-tank/quality-news — NOT PubMed. ChatGPT q1 cites the Treasury Board cloud-guardrail Protected-B contracts (`:25`) and the Hydro-Québec 13¢/kWh tariff filing (`:23`); q2 leans on `fao-on.org`, `bankofcanada.ca`, `cdhowe.org`, `ustr.gov`, `pm.gc.ca`, and law-firm CUSMA explainers `blakes.com`/`mccarthy.ca`/`torys.com` (`compare_gemini_q2.md:292-528`); q3 on `www150.statcan.gc.ca`, `oecd.org`, `cdhowe.org`, `nber.org` (`compare_gemini_q3.md:244-397`). For ECONOMICS, the press-release/think-tank/analyst tier IS the legitimate evidence base — the opposite of the clinical lesson.

**1.5 HONEST, FENCED uncertainty — the place POLARIS can WIN.** ChatGPT labels every synthetic number: *"the illustrative capex assumption is US$45,000 per H100-equivalent... These are modeling assumptions, not quoted federal procurement prices"* (`compare_chatgpt_q1.md:36`) and names its biggest gap: *"The biggest evidence gap is around specific Canadian sovereign frontier clusters... did not provide enough detail... to model a separate 'SCALE AI cluster' line item"* (`:143`). Gemini OVERCLAIMS unhedged specifics: *"TELUS... a cluster projected to scale to an astonishing 60,000 high-performance GPUs and 150 megawatts... a $9 billion economic injection"* and *"Alpha Compute officially activated the ALPHA-01 cluster... 504 of the latest NVIDIA B200 GPUs"* and *"3.6 million units heavily backordered through mid-2026"* (`compare_gemini_q1.md:7`) — stated as fact, no hedge, no recoverable anchor in the q1 export.

**1.6 Citation faithfulness when anchors survive — the real bar, traced.** The q1/q2/q4/q5 `.md` exports have superscripts STRIPPED (capture artifact); "Gemini doesn't cite" is the WRONG read. Traced through `compare_gemini_dr.txt` (42 numbered refs preserved): SURPASS-CVOT is cited [30]→`pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41406444/` and reproduces *"12.2% (801 patients)... versus 13.1% (862 patients)... Hazard Ratio 0.92; 95.3% CI, 0.83 to 1.01; P = 0.003"* — an exact match to the primary source, including the unusual 95.3% CI width (a fabricator gets that wrong). BUT Gemini routinely cites manufacturer PR and trade news (`investor.lilly.com`, `prnewswire.com`, `healio.com`, `pharmacytimes.com`) for trial numbers rather than the NEJM/Lancet primary. ChatGPT's dr.txt reaches the regulatory primary — `pi.lilly.com/us/mounjaro-uspi.pdf`, `accessdata.fda.gov/.../215866Orig1s000StatR.pdf`, `ema.europa.eu/.../mounjaro-epar-product-information_en.pdf` (`compare_chatgpt_dr.txt:1129-1226`) — and makes the cleanest limitation statement of the four: *"the conclusion here is inferential, not based on a definitive modern head-to-head RCT at semaglutide 2 mg"* (`:751-762`).

**The bar in one line:** a navigable, question-shaped report with a quantified trade-off + a decision artifact + real government/analyst sourcing + per-number honesty about what is modeled vs sourced. Length is not the bar; faithfulness + decision-usefulness is.

## 4. HONEST POSITION vs FRONTIER on a BROAD question

**Where POLARIS can WIN (after Steps 1-5):**
- **Verifiable per-claim sourcing.** strict_verify (provenance token + numeric-match + content-overlap, `provenance_generator.py:957-1291`) is domain-AGNOSTIC and kills exactly Gemini's two worst broad-question habits: unhedged invented specifics (q1 "60,000 GPUs / ALPHA-01 / 3.6M backordered", `compare_gemini_q1.md:7`) and citing manufacturer PR / vendor-optimism aggregates as fact (q3 "$180B by 2030" from Meta/Microsoft vendor pages, `compare_gemini_q3.md:248-252`). POLARIS structurally cannot emit a number that isn't in a cited span.
- **Refusal / fail-closed honesty.** `abort_no_verified_sections` and the corpus-approval gate mean POLARIS refuses to fabricate a report from a thin corpus — the opposite of Gemini stating future-dated "facts" ($653k 2026 home price, `compare_gemini_q4.md:56`) flat.
- **Conflict-surfacing + modeled-vs-sourced labeling.** The 4-role verify (Writer/Mirror/Sentinel/Judge) can enforce ChatGPT's discipline of labeling every modeled number ("modeling assumptions, not quoted procurement prices", `compare_chatgpt_q1.md:36`) as a hard rule, which Gemini does not.

**Where POLARIS is at PARITY (achievable, not guaranteed):**
- **Prose quality + coverage.** DeepSeek-V4-Pro can produce analyst-grade narrative; parity depends on Step 4 (section model) + Step 6 (depth caps) landing. The frontier's 5-9k-word coverage is reachable once the corpus is real and the section model is question-shaped.
- **The decision artifact (comparison table / risk matrix).** POLARIS has no structural barrier to emitting these once the section blueprint is domain-general; it has never done so because the blueprint is clinical.

**Where POLARIS currently LOSES and MUST be fixed first:**
- **Source reach on broad policy (Gaps B+C).** Today it retrieves arXiv/GitHub (Q1 misroute) or US-only policy filters (Q4) and mis-tiers the Canadian authorities as UNKNOWN. It cannot assemble the CSIS/Bank-of-Canada/FAO/USTR/CD-Howe corpus the frontier relied on. THIS is the hard loss; everything else is downstream.
- **Question-shaped structure (Gap E).** A clinical Efficacy/Safety/Dose-Response report on a housing question is an automatic loss.
- **The quantified trade-off + sensitivity (Bar 1.2).** POLARIS has NO cost-model/break-even/sensitivity capability; the contract architecture models trial entities, not cost curves. This is the largest CAPABILITY gap (not just a parameter) — even after Steps 1-6, matching ChatGPT's "beats AWS above 32% utilization + 6-row sensitivity table" requires the Writer to do quantitative modeling from retrieved inputs, which is a prompt/section-design problem to validate empirically, not a guaranteed win.
