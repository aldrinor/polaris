# POLARIS Source-Credibility & Multi-Perspective Recommendations Report\n### Driving two revisions to `docs/credibility_weighted_sourcing_redesign_plan_2026_06_07.md` — LAYER 1 (scoring) and LAYER 6 (composition + UI)\n\n**Author:** Lead analyst (Claude). **Date:** 2026-06-08.\n**Inputs:** three research streams (per-question adaptive credibility weighting; neutral multi-perspective presentation; friendly UI/artifact patterns).\n**Status:** revision driver. Each layer below states what it **REPLACES** in the Codex-APPROVE'd 2026-06-07 plan, then the concrete mechanism, auditability, reliability, and POLARIS wiring.\n\n**Invariants preserved across both revisions** (these are not negotiable and the revisions do not weaken them): the LLM credibility-judge is **advisory only** — `strict_verify`'s six per-sentence checks (`provenance_generator.py:1156`) remain the binding faithfulness gate; every new layer is **flag-gated, default-OFF byte-identical**, fail-loud on missing inputs; weight is **evidence quality/tier, never headcount**; every displayed aggregate is **one click from the cited `[#ev:id:start-end]` span and its VERIFIED/PARTIAL verdict**; the credibility prior stays **self-computed and verifier-overridable** (no single external rater hardwired); independence-collapse (`synthesis/independence_collapse.py`) still runs before any weighted tally; the contested-topic adjudication **reuses the existing 4-role / voter-arbiter pipeline** (`roles/role_pipeline.py` + `honest_sweep_integration.py`), open-weight voters only — no parallel path is built.\n\n---\n\n## 1. LAYER 1 (scoring): adaptive per-question credibility-skill, replacing the fixed domain-rubric selector\n\n### 1.1 What this REPLACES\n\nThe 2026-06-07 plan's **Phase 2a/2b (L1)** selects one of **three fixed reliability rubrics** (clinical → RCT+peer-review+RoB; econ/policy → design-validity; qualitative → CERQual) keyed off the detected domain. That is still a small lookup table: domain → rubric. The operator constraint is explicit — **replace fixed domain rubrics** with a **generic, domain-agnostic credibility skill the LLM applies by reasoning per question**. Research stream #1's central finding is that the frontier (OpenAI, Gemini, Claude) has already moved this way, and that Perplexity's baked-in EEAT/domain-authority scoring is the closest-to-forbidden counter-example (gameable, popularity-biased, ~60% Google-overlap). We copy the reasoned-generic camp.\n\nThe revision: **domain stops being a selector and becomes context that primes one generic skill.** There is exactly one credibility skill; the detected `scope_template` domain is passed in as a hint the skill reasons over ("this is a clinical efficacy question, so weight trial design and RoB heavily"), not a branch that swaps rubrics. This scales to 10,000+ fields with zero per-field config.\n\n### 1.2 The exact mechanism — credibility-skill / guardrail prompt × (reliability × relevance)\n\n**Step A — deterministic signals (POLARIS produces these; unchanged substrate).** `authority_model.score_source_authority` and `tier_classifier` already emit, per source, the field-agnostic primitives: `authority_score [0,1]`, `source_class`, `corroboration_count` (independent-host agreement), `authority_confidence`, `signal_scores` (the A–E sub-scores), plus `junk_class` / `predatory_oa` (`source_class.py:71-89`). Independence-collapse (`independence_collapse.py`) emits `origin_cluster_id` + canonical-origin designation + copy flag. **None of this is replaced.** These are the inspectable, host-name-free, self-computed inputs (LAW VI: zero host literals in code).\n\n**Step B — the credibility skill consumes those signals and weights them per question.** A new pure prompt module, `authority/credibility_skill.py`, runs a **JudgeRank-style 3-step decomposition** (arXiv 2411.00142) — chosen because each step is inspectable text and is itself the audit template:\n\n1. **Query analysis** — identify what THIS question actually needs (the claim type, what would count as strong evidence for it).\n2. **Source analysis** — for each candidate source, a query-AWARE assessment applying **generic SIFT + lateral-reading + provenance heuristics**, NOT a domain table. The skill's instruction block encodes the domain-agnostic primacy heuristics that are OpenAI's own deep-research pattern: *prefer the primary/official source over an aggregator or SEO blog; prefer the original paper over a survey/secondary summary; corroborate laterally (does independent coverage agree); weigh source incentives/track record; downgrade stale-but-authoritative and retracted/superseded* (the latter wires to the existing `supersession.py` Phase 3 signal).\n3. **Two-axis judgment with rationale** — emit, per source, **RELIABILITY × RELEVANCE** (the EFSA/OHAT weight-of-evidence two-axis, which POLARIS currently conflates into one tier): reliability = internal validity/method given what the skill read in the signals; relevance = directness to this specific question. The product is the per-question credibility weight. **Crucially, the skill is told its priors are the `authority_model` signals and that it may up- or down-weight them with a stated reason** — it does not invent authority from nothing, and it cannot silently override `authority_confidence=LOW` into a fabricated HIGH (thin OpenAlex/ROR signal stays honestly LOW).\n\n**Output schema (machine-parseable, bound to the schema discipline already in §8.3.9):** per source — `{reliability_score, relevance_score, credibility_weight, rationale_string, signals_cited:[...]}`. The weight is **a visible, revisable rationale string, never a hidden scalar and never a fine-tuned-in behavior** (this is why we reject the CAG fine-tune approach from stream #1: opaque learned weights are exactly the auditability failure POLARIS differentiates against — borrow CAG's *evidence* that credibility-conditioning gives noise robustness, and its three generic dimensions of relevance/timeliness/reliability, but implement them at inference time as a prompt-visible signal).\n\n### 1.3 How it stays AUDITABLE (auditable by construction, not bolted on)\n\nThe judgment IS a reasoning trace, so it is emitted verbatim and **bound to the same provenance substrate POLARIS already has**:\n\n- The per-source `credibility_weight` + `rationale_string` + `signals_cited` attach to the source's `AuthorityResult` as **additive fields** (mirroring how `signal_scores`, `junk_class`, `predatory_oa` are already additive side-outputs on the dataclass), emitted on rows in `tier_classifier.py`.\n- At claim time, the weight flows into the four Phase-1/Phase-8 disclosure fields on `SentenceVerification` (`credibility_weight`, `independent_origin_count`, `certainty_label`, `span_verdict`), so each verified sentence shows **which source was weighted how, and why**, one click from its `[#ev]` span.\n- Because the skill cites the deterministic `signals_cited` it reasoned from, the rationale is **checkable against the inputs** — a reviewer can confirm "preferred A over B because A is the primary trial-registry entry and B a secondary news summary" against the actual `source_class` values. The rationale is a **claim to verify, not ground truth** (post-hoc rationalization risk per stream #1) — so it is advisory and the binding gate stays `strict_verify`.\n\nThis is the §-1.1-compliant story: no metadata/count/string-presence signal, every weight traceable to a cited span and an inspectable reason.\n\n### 1.4 Reliability and where it fails (and the guardrail)\n\nStream #1 is blunt about the failure modes, and they bound the design: LLMs "treat all retrieved documents as equally valid" unless explicitly steered and over-rely on high-frequency claims (arXiv 2505.17762); AuthorityBench (2603.25092) shows authority perception is **inconsistent across domains and highly prompt-sensitive**, struggles with false authority, and exhibits position/verbosity/self-preference bias. Therefore:\n\n- **The skill is ADVISORY to ranking/selection, never the faithfulness gate.** It changes *which rows are billed/weighted*, not *whether a sentence verifies its span*. `strict_verify`, 4-role/two-family segregation, and the independent `corpus_approval_gate` tier-distribution backstop all still run sequentially.\n- **Bias mitigations baked into the prompt:** score rationale-first then number; randomize candidate order to blunt position bias; run pointwise (per-source) to avoid listwise permutation instability.\n- **Offline validation before trust:** the chosen judge model+prompt is validated on an **AuthorityBench-style harness** (authority-PERCEPTION only, per the §6a no-overstatement correction — not as a volume-vulnerability benchmark) plus the existing vax adversarial fixture, before it is allowed on the shipping path. Calibration (Brier/ECE) on the emitted weights runs in the existing Phase 11 harness.\n- **Capture resistance:** multi-signal, self-computed, verifier-overridable — no NewsGuard/MBFC/Ad Fontes verdict is hardwired as ground truth (the FTC-capture risk).\n\n### 1.5 The honest gap + exactly what POLARIS builds for L1\n\n**HAVE:** deterministic authority signals (`authority_model`, `tier_classifier`), the additive `AuthorityResult`/`SentenceVerification` schema, independence-collapse, `scope_templates` domain detection, the JSON-verdict schema discipline.\n**MISSING:** the generic per-question credibility skill itself, and the second (relevance) axis.\n**BUILD (revision to Phase 2a/2b):**\n1. `authority/credibility_skill.py` — the pure 3-step (query→source→two-axis-judgment) prompt module emitting `{reliability, relevance, credibility_weight, rationale, signals_cited}`. Domain is an input *hint*, not a rubric switch. (Replaces the fixed-rubric selector in `credibility_prior.py`.)\n2. Add `relevance_score` (and the rationale fields) to `AuthorityResult` (`source_class.py:75`); emit on rows in `tier_classifier.py`.\n3. Wire the skill's weight into the L5 weight-mass tally and the L7 disclosure fields — advisory input, not a gate.\n4. Flag-gated default-OFF byte-identical; OFF = the legacy single-scalar path, byte-for-byte.\n\n---\n\n## 2. LAYER 6 (composition + UI): neutral multi-perspective + transparent evidence-weight artifact, replacing the forewarning/fringe framing\n\n### 2.1 What this REPLACES\n\nThe 2026-06-07 plan's **Phase 7 (L6)** composes contested claims as "consensus at high weight + minority attributed at low weight **with explicit forewarning**, medical-misinfo **low-weight/fringe**, ABSTAIN over fabricated balance." The operator directive is to **replace the forewarning/fringe framing** with a **neutral multi-perspective presentation where competing world-views are shown as legitimate, each carrying its real (possibly asymmetric) evidence weight, and the user judges** — no bias, no stamps, no forewarning prose.\n\n### 2.2 The hard tension, named and resolved (do not skip this)\n\nThere is a real conflict between two binding constraints, and both sides have a lethal failure mode:\n\n- The operator wants **no fringe/forewarning stamp, all sides legitimate, no bias.**\n- The **clinical-safety invariant #5** (binding, preserved) plus Dixon-Clarke 2013 (PubMed 23193194, measured harm: falsely-balanced vaccine reporting *reduced* perceived safety and vaccination intent), Wikipedia FRINGE, and §-1.1 all say **equal treatment of unequal evidence is itself bias** and is "lethal in clinical." I am not authorized to drop invariant #5.\n\n**The reconciliation — handed to us nearly verbatim by all three streams: replace the LABEL with the BASIS.** Wikipedia NPOV's "neutral *editing*, not neutral *content*"; GRADE's "show the basis, let the user infer"; the no-warning rule from stream #2 is satisfiable **if and only if the criteria behind any weight are displayed**. So:\n\n- The **system** stamps nothing — no "fringe," no "debunked," no forewarning sentence. That is the neutrality.\n- The **evidence weight** carries the (often asymmetric) truth, rendered as a visible **per-perspective evidence-basis panel** the user reads and judges. The asymmetry — *14 RCTs + 3 systematic reviews [High]* vs *2 case reports + mechanistic argument [Very Low]* — becomes **visible in the artifact** instead of **asserted in a stamp**. The user infers; the system does not editorialize.\n- "Legitimate" means **shown with its real (possibly low) weight and one-click drill-down to span + verdict** — NOT "shown as equally supported." A genuine world-view dispute with no empirical consensus (values, forecasting) shows steelmanned sides at honest parity; an empirical dispute with consensus shows the real weight distribution.\n- The medical-misinfo case is handled **without the banned word**: its panel is simply **near-empty of independent high-tier basis**, and where there is no independent consensus the claim **ABSTAINs** ("contested; no independent consensus" — reusing existing refuse/unresolved behavior). This preserves invariant #5's *substance* (not normalized as an equal side) while dropping the stamp.\n\nThis is neutral AND anti-false-balance AND §-1.1-safe simultaneously — the operator's "let the user judge" satisfied by *showing the basis*, not by *equalizing the boxes*.\n\n### 2.3 The exact composition mechanism\n\nPer question, the composition path first **diagnoses the contestation type** (this IS the adaptive guardrail, copied from Anthropic's even-handedness "lack-of-consensus" discriminator + OpenAI's seek-the-truth-together):\n\n- **empirical-with-consensus** (vaccines, climate) → due-weight: each side shown, weight proportional to verified-evidence quality; the asymmetry is the message, carried by the basis panel.\n- **genuine-uncertainty** (open scientific questions) → separate *supported* from *open*; sides at honest parity.\n- **values dispute** (politics, ethics) → **steelman each legitimate view** (Ideological Turing Test, neutral terminology, no verdict); no evidence-weight asymmetry asserted because there is no due weight to defer to.\n- **forecasting** (AI-and-jobs) → scenario spread, projective not factual.\n\nEach perspective is gathered through its **own retrieval lens** (MODS-style per-perspective retrieval + citation, arXiv 2502.00322 — raw LLMs silently drop minority views; per-perspective retrieval prevents the drop), then composed via the **existing 4-role / voter-arbiter agreement** (cross-lineage, open-weight voters — the bridging/balanced-panel analog) — **not a new adjudication path**. Reranking-for-balance (arXiv 2506.15925) selects the most balanced faithful candidate, because direct just-prompt-balanced generation empirically skews. **The weight attached to each perspective is evidence QUALITY × independent-origin count, never document count** — else we rebuild Ground News's source-count bias inside the summarizer.\n\n### 2.4 The friendly UI artifact — design\n\nBuilt on the **components that already exist** (#744 citation chip + hover source card, #745 per-claim verdict chip, #747 Proof Replay split-view, #757 report page, #758 claim-anchored compare view) — not designed from scratch.\n\n**The artifact: a claim-anchored "Perspectives" panel** that opens from any contested sentence (reusing the compare-view host), containing per legitimate perspective:\n\n1. **A neutral position header** — "Position held by [who], with this evidence behind it" — no "fringe"/"warning"/"debunked". A steelmanned one-line statement of the view in neutral terms.\n2. **An evidence-basis sub-panel (GRADE-style, the load-bearing element):** the study types / source tiers / independent-origin count behind that perspective, rendered as **frequencies, not a percentage or a lone dot** — "*8 of n: RCTs + systematic reviews [High certainty]*" vs "*2 of n: case reports + mechanistic argument [Very Low]*". The certainty label is acceptable **only because its downgrade criteria are shown** (study design / consistency / directness / independence). This makes the asymmetry self-evident without a stamp.\n3. **Scite-style polarity badges** (supporting / contrasting / contextualizing) on each cited source — **neutral hue + icon + text label, never color alone** (colorblind safety; red=contrasting reads as red=wrong and biases toward consensus — explicitly avoided).\n4. **Drill-down:** every badge, every basis count, every panel is **one click from the raw `[#ev]` span and its VERIFIED/PARTIAL verdict** — the Scite "self-correcting" property: a mis-classified badge is exposed by the citing sentence sitting right behind it, and a meter without drill-down would violate the §-1.1 metadata-audit ban.\n\n**Panel sizing defeats false balance structurally, not with a caveat:** panels are **sized/annotated by real verified-evidence weight**, not equal-width — because equal real estate reads as equal credibility (the false-balance trap), and a caveat is empirically insufficient (Effectiviology / false-balance literature). The asymmetry is *in the layout and the basis panel*, not asserted in prose.\n\n**Three named anti-patterns explicitly rejected** (all from the streams, all as negative templates):\n- **Ground News source-count / coverage bar** — measures who-is-saying-this (volume), not evidence weight; §-1.1 bans source counts as quality signals. If a composition strip is shown at all, its axis is **evidence tier / source type**, never ideology or headcount, and it is kept visually separate from the weight panel so "many sources" never reads as "true."\n- **Bare confidence/credibility score (% or traffic-light dot)** — the best-documented HARM pattern: overconfident displays (80% shown / 70% accurate) pushed AI-advice adoption 57%→69.6%, 64% of users mis-judged calibration, and in a 2025 breast-cancer CDSS high confidence *reduced* diagnostic accuracy via overreliance. POLARIS's honest replacement is the per-sentence VERIFIED/PARTIAL verdict against a real span — never a glanceable dot.\n- **Symmetric 50/50 equal-box presentation** — manufactures false balance; the Dixon-Clarke measured harm.\n\n### 2.5 The honest gap + exactly what POLARIS builds for L6\n\n**HAVE:** the disclosure substrate (per-sentence `[#ev]` tokens, `strict_verify` verdict ladder, `SentenceVerification` additive fields), the existing 4-role/voter-arbiter agreement, independence-collapse for origin-count, and the full UI component kit (citation chip, verdict chip, Proof Replay, compare view).\n**MISSING:** the contestation-type classifier, per-perspective retrieval + composition that defeats false balance by weight, and the Perspectives artifact rendering basis panels.\n**BUILD (revision to Phase 7):**\n1. A **contestation-type classifier** (empirical-consensus / genuine-uncertainty / values / forecasting) priming the composition rule — replaces the fixed forewarning+fringe rule.\n2. **Per-perspective retrieval + balance-rerank composition** over the post-collapse origins, weighting each perspective by verified-evidence quality × independent-origin count (not count).\n3. A **steelman + GRADE-style evidence-basis renderer** feeding a new **claim-anchored "Perspectives" panel** in the compare-view host: neutral headers, frequency-framed basis sub-panels, Scite-style neutral polarity badges, full span-verdict drill-down, weight-proportional sizing.\n4. Validated with a **paired-prompt neutrality eval** (Anthropic's open-sourced political-neutrality harness, adapted so it does NOT reward equal treatment of unequal evidence) + the vax adversarial fixture (asserts the asymmetry renders, no stamp, no 50/50).\n5. Flag-gated default-OFF byte-identical; ABSTAIN reuses existing refuse/unresolved behavior; forewarning prose removed, replaced by the basis panel.\n\n---\n\n## 3.
