Treatment with tirzepatide led to significant weight loss in adults with obesity or overweight with weight-related comorbidities (excluding type 2 diabetes), according to results from the phase 3 SURMOUNT-3 and SURMOUNT-4 studies. Tirzepatide is an investigational once-weekly, dual glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide and glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonist that integrates the actions of both incretins into a single molecule. The multicenter, randomized, double-blind, parallel, placebo-controlled SURMOUNT-3 trial (ClinicalTrials.gov Identifier: [NCT04657016](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT04657016?term=NCT04657016&rank=1)) evaluated the efficacy and safety of tirzepatide for 72 weeks after a 12-week intensive lifestyle intervention lead-in period in adults with obesity or overweight with weight-related comorbidities, excluding type 2 diabetes. At the end of the 12-week period, study participants (N=579) achieved 6.9% mean weight loss and were randomly assigned 1:1 to receive tirzepatide or placebo subcutaneously once weekly. The efficacy estimand (efficacy prior to discontinuation of study drug) results demonstrated that treatment with tirzepatide was associated with an additional 21.1% reduction in body weight over 72 weeks vs a 3.3% weight regain for the placebo group.
