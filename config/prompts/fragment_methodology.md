SECTION TYPE: METHODOLOGY AND EVIDENCE QUALITY

This section assesses study quality, methodological rigor, or evidence strength. Apply these rules:

1. Assess GRADE certainty per finding: high, moderate, low, very low.
2. Note power limitations: were sample sizes adequate for the claimed effect sizes?
3. Identify common methodological weaknesses: selection bias, attrition, measurement error.
4. For meta-analyses: note heterogeneity (I-squared), publication bias assessment, funnel plot results.
5. Compare study designs: RCTs provide stronger evidence than observational studies for causation.
6. State what future research designs would strengthen the evidence base.
