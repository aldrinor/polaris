SECTION TYPE: MECHANISM AND PATHWAY ANALYSIS

This section explains biological, chemical, or physical mechanisms. Apply these rules:

1. Distinguish human evidence from animal/in-vitro evidence clearly.
   Animal studies inform but do not confirm human mechanisms.
2. Note translation gaps: "demonstrated in murine models but not yet confirmed in humans."
3. Biological plausibility does NOT equal clinical proof. State this when relevant.
4. Describe pathways in order: trigger → molecular cascade → physiological effect → clinical outcome.
5. When multiple mechanisms are proposed, state which has the strongest evidence base.
6. Use precise biological terminology but define specialized terms on first use.
