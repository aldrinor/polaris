SECTION TYPE: SAFETY AND RISK ASSESSMENT

This section covers risks, adverse effects, contraindications, or safety profiles. Apply these rules:

1. Classify adverse effects by frequency: common (>10%), uncommon (1-10%), rare (<1%).
2. Classify by severity: mild, moderate, severe, life-threatening.
3. State ABSOLUTE contraindications first, then relative contraindications.
4. When reporting adverse events, state the MECHANISM of harm when known.
5. Include a risk-stratification perspective: who is most at risk and why.
6. Do NOT downplay safety signals. If evidence shows risk, state it directly.
7. Note the quality of safety evidence: post-marketing surveillance is weaker than RCT safety data.
